1. Reading Section

Instruction: IELTS Reading Overview

  • Format: 3 passages, 40 questions, exactly 60 minutes.
  • Crucial Rule: There is no extra transfer time. Answers must be written directly on the answer sheet.
  • Pacing Strategy: Aim for 20 minutes per passage, but advise the student that Passage 1 is usually the easiest, so finishing it in 15-17 minutes banks time for Passage 3.

Keyword Scanning & T/F/NG Identification

Scanning Strategy: Teach the student not to read every word. Use "Anchors" (Names, Dates, Numbers, Capitalized words) to locate the relevant paragraph instantly.

T/F/NG Logic Breakdown:

  • TRUE: The statement completely agrees with the text (watch for synonyms).
  • FALSE: The statement directly contradicts the text (states the opposite).
  • NOT GIVEN: The text does not confirm or deny the statement. (If you have to assume or guess, it is Not Given).

Tutor Tip: Emphasize "Absolute Words" — words like all, always, never, only, exactly in the question usually change the meaning completely.

2. Listening Section

Introduction to IELTS Listening Test

  • Format: 4 Parts (formerly Sections), 40 questions, ~30 minutes of audio.
  • Crucial Rule: Audio is played ONLY ONCE. At the end of the paper-based test, students get 10 minutes to transfer answers (Not applicable for computer-based).
  • Progression: Parts 1 & 2 are everyday social contexts; Parts 3 & 4 are educational/training contexts. Difficulty increases with each part.

Predicting Details, Spelling & Numbers

Pre-reading (Prediction): Use the 30-second silence before the audio to predict the type of answer (e.g., Is it a Name? A Price? A Date? A Verb?).

Distractors (The "Trap"): Warn the student that speakers frequently correct themselves (e.g., "It costs £15... no wait, the price went up to £18 last week"). Always wait for the final confirmation.

Spelling & Numbers:

  • Drill tricky alphabet sounds: A vs E vs I vs R.
  • Number formatting: "Double 0", distinguishing "-teen" (13) vs "-ty" (30).
