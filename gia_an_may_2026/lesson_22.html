2. Speaking Part 2 & 3 Practice

Topic Resource List

Strategic Guidance: Upgrading Band 5.5 to Band 6.5

Ensure the student presents coherent, structured responses without long pauses when discussing personal health habits and broader lifestyle issues:

  • Part 2 Storytelling: Structure monologues using chronological markers (*"In the beginning...", "What I usually do is...", "The main reason why this helps me..."*).
  • Part 3 A.R.E.A Framework: Maintain strict response flow: Direct Answer [A] → Reason [R] → General Example [E] → Alternative/Conditional [A].
  • Natural Collocations: Focus on practical, natural phrases like *"balanced diet"*, *"reduce stress"*, *"stay in shape"*, *"junk food tax"*.

Part 2 Cue Cards (Click to inspect Band 6.5 Model Stories)

CUE CARD A

Describe a healthy lifestyle you know about.

  • • Whose lifestyle it is?
  • • What this lifestyle involves?
  • • How easy or difficult it is to follow?
  • • And explain why you think this lifestyle is healthy.
CUE CARD B

Describe an outdoor activity you do to stay healthy.

  • • What the activity is?
  • • Where and when you do it?
  • • Who you do it with?
  • • And explain why you think it helps you stay healthy.
CUE CARD C

Describe a time you felt very stressed and how you relaxed.

  • • When it happened and why you felt stressed?
  • • What you did to relax?
  • • Who helped you or gave you advice?
  • • And explain how you felt after doing this activity.

[A - Answer] In my opinion, fast food is extremely popular mainly because it is convenient and affordable for busy workers.

[R - Reason] Many people today work long hours and do not have enough energy or time to buy fresh ingredients and cook meals at home.

[E - Example] For instance, students and office workers in big cities often grab quick burgers or fried chicken during short lunch breaks.

[A - Alternative] If healthy restaurants served quick meals at lower prices, more people would definitely choose healthier options.

[A - Answer] Yes, I believe introducing a tax on sugary products is a effective way to encourage healthier dietary choices.

[R - Reason] Higher prices naturally discourage consumers from buying junk food frequently, while tax money can fund healthcare facilities.

[E - Example] For example, countries like the UK implemented a sugar tax on soft drinks, which led companies to reduce sugar levels in their products.

[A - Alternative] However, if governments only raise taxes without educating citizens about nutrition, people might still buy unhealthy snacks anyway.

[A - Answer] Overall, modern diets contain far more processed items and red meat than in the past.

[R - Reason] Fifty years ago, families relied heavily on fresh, locally grown vegetables, whereas today food processing and global trade dominate market shelves.

[E - Example] For instance, instead of eating home-cooked rice and fresh fish, many families now eat pre-packaged frozen meals or canned foods.

[A - Alternative] On the positive side, awareness of organic diets and vegetarian lifestyles is growing rapidly among young urbanites today.

[A - Answer] I think the primary reason is a lack of time combined with unrealistic initial expectations.

[R - Reason] People often set overly difficult goals at first, so when they feel exhausted after a long workday, they easily give up.

[E - Example] For instance, many people buy expensive gym memberships in January, but stop attending after just a few weeks because they lack motivation.

[A - Alternative] If individuals start with short 15-minute daily walks, it becomes much easier to form a long-term habit.

[A - Answer] In my view, exercising outdoors in nature is generally better for both physical and mental well-being.

[R - Reason] Outdoor environments provide fresh oxygen, natural sunlight, and a peaceful atmosphere that helps relieve mental stress.

[E - Example] For example, jogging in a green park helps people clear their minds much better than running on a treadmill in a noisy room.

[A - Alternative] However, during extremely hot or rainy weather, indoor gyms provide essential climate control and specialized equipment.
