1. Writing Task 2 | Discussion Essays

Deconstructing "Discuss both views & give your opinion"

Golden Structure Rules for Discussion Prompts

Discussion Essays require a highly objective structural execution. The student must thoroughly evaluate both perspectives before delivering their own stance to secure Band 7.0+ in Task Achievement (TA).

The Balanced 4-Paragraph Model

1. Introduction: Paraphrase the debate and state a clear, direct thesis presenting your opinion.

2. Body Paragraph A (View 1): Discuss View 1 objectively. State *why* people hold this perspective, using general/impersonal examples.

3. Body Paragraph B (View 2 & Opinion): Discuss View 2, showing why you find it stronger. Integrate your own view smoothly here.

4. Conclusion: Re-summarize both standpoints and firmly restate your final personal conclusion.

Key Tutoring Milestones

Avoid Early Bias: Teach the student not to dismiss View 1 immediately in Body A. Use phrases like: "Proponents of this idea argue that..." or "This view is often supported by those who believe..."

Distinct Personal Voice: Keep the student's personal view aligned with their thesis throughout. This is vital to prevent "shifting perspectives" which penalizes Coherence & Cohesion (CC).

Academic Discussion Phrase Bank (Band 7.0+ Target)
Introduction & Thesis Formulas

• "While compelling arguments can be marshaled on both sides, my firm conviction lies with..."

• "The debate surrounding [X] is highly multifaceted, with one camp championing... while opponents maintain..."

• "This essay will critically evaluate both perspectives before arguing that..."

Evaluating Third-Party Views (Body A)

• "Proponents of this standpoint argue that..." (Vital for maintaining impersonal objectivity)

• "This view is often supported by those who advocate for..."

• "The primary justification offered by supporters is that..."

Transitioning to Personal Opinion (Body B)

• "Nonetheless, there are highly persuasive grounds to suggest that..."

• "Despite the validity of the aforementioned argument, I am inclined to support the view that..."

• "A far more profound perspective, however, focuses on..."

Topical Framework: Health & Lifestyle

Health Vocab Sheet

Break down these three real-test health and education prompts. Guide the student to map reasons, examples, and target vocabulary for each standpoint.

1

"Some people believe that governments should limit working hours to improve people's health and well-being. Others think individuals should be free to decide how many hours they work."

View A: Govt Intervention

Prevents employee exploitation, combats occupational burnout, reduces cardiovascular conditions linked to chronic overwork.

View B: Individual Autonomy

Promotes financial flexibility, allows personal career progression, recognizes different financial baselines.

2

"Some people think schools should place greater emphasis on teaching students about mental health and emotional well-being. Others believe academic subjects should remain the main focus of education."

View A: Emotional/Mental Curriculums

Addresses youth anxiety epidemics, provides coping mechanisms for stress, reduces downstream clinical conditions.

View B: Pure Academic Priority

Ensures economic readiness, supports standardized performance metrics, keeps mental care as a parental duty.

3

"Some people believe that modern cities make it difficult for residents to maintain a healthy lifestyle. Others think city living offers more opportunities for health and fitness than living in rural areas."

View A: City lifestyle barriers

High air pollution, light/noise pollution disrupts circadian sleep rhythm, sedentary desk-bound occupations.

View B: Urban wellness amenities

State-of-the-art gyms, walkability networks (parks, trails), concentrated access to medical care and organic produce.
