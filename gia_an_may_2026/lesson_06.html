2. Reading Section

Deep Dive: False vs. Not Given

The Core Challenge: In Passage 3, texts are abstract and heavily paraphrased. Students frequently confuse "False" with "Not Given" because they rely on outside knowledge or over-infer.

FALSE / NO

The passage states the opposite or explicitly contradicts the statement. You must be able to point to the exact words that make it impossible.

NOT GIVEN

The passage might mention the topic, but the specific claim is missing. If you have to say "Well, probably...", then it is Not Given.

3. Listening Section

Part 3 MCQs: Academic Discussions & Distractors

The Context: Part 3 involves up to 3 speakers discussing an academic topic (e.g., an assignment). It tests the ability to follow changing opinions and agreements.

Handling Long Multiple Choice:

  • Read the Question Core: Highlight the main verb/noun in the prompt before the audio starts. What exactly are you listening for?
  • The "Distractor" Trap: The audio will almost always mention words from every A, B, and C option. Do not pick an answer just because you heard a matching word.
  • Listen for Resolution: Speakers will disagree or change their minds ("I thought so too, but actually..."). Wait for the final agreement marker ("Yes, let's go with that.") before finalizing the answer.
