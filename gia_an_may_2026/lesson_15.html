1. Writing Task 1: Maps Description

Strategic Guidance: Navigating Structural Alterations Over Time

Map prompts examine a student's ability to report physical layout transformations. Grouping coordinates cleanly is vital for high Band Coherence (CC).

Standard 4-Paragraph Layout:

1. Introduction: Paraphrase the description parameters, identifying the locations and specific years compared.

2. Overview: Emphasize the macro-level transformation trend (e.g., from simple, traditional configurations to specialized, high-capacity zones). Do not list isolated changes here.

3. Detail Body 1: Detail the changes inside specific zones (e.g., the western and northern boundaries).

4. Detail Body 2: Detail the changes across the remaining regions (e.g., the center, eastern, and southern areas).

Static Map Language Reference

Addition & Construction
  • "...was constructed/erected..."
  • "...witnessed the introduction of..."
  • "An additional facility was added..."
  • "...was newly built/installed..."
Demolition & Replacement
  • "...was demolished/cleared..."
  • "...was knocked down to make way for..."
  • "...replaced / was converted into..."
  • "...redeveloped into a specialized..."
Spatial Prepositions
  • "...running along the northern wall..."
  • "...situated in the south-west corner..."
  • "...located adjacent/opposite to..."
  • "...positioned in the central space..."

2. Writing Task 1: Process Flowcharts

Strategic Guidance: Passive Voice & Temporal Sequencers

Process flow diagrams describe step-by-step systems. Since the actions are performed on objects rather than by agents, using the passive voice is critical to securing a Band 7.5+ in Grammar (GRA).

Key Flowchart Guidelines:

1. Passive Transformations: Convert simple active indicators on diagrams into passive actions (e.g., instead of saying "filter cleans water", say "raw water is passed through a filter where impurities are removed").

2. Stage Transitions: Connect stages seamlessly. Avoid overusing basic connectors like "and then", "next". Leverage advanced sequencers ("Subsequently,", "Following the carbonation stage,", "Once filtration is finalized,").

3. Initial & Final States: Ensure the introduction and overview specify the exact starting ingredient/raw input and the final output destination.

Process Flow Language Reference

Stage Sequencers
  • "Initially / At the outset,"
  • "In the subsequent stage..."
  • "Following this filtration process,"
  • "Simultaneously / Concurrently,"
Passive Structures
  • "...is heated / cooled..."
  • "...are sequentially pumped..."
  • "...is heavily mixed with..."
  • "...is distributed via..."
Stage Links & Causes
  • "...thereby allowing..."
  • "...prior to being directed..."
  • "...resulting in the formation of..."
  • "...once this step is finalized..."
