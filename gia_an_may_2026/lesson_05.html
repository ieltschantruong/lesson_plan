2. Reading Section

Matching Headings vs. Matching Features

Matching Headings

"Choose the correct heading for each paragraph"

  • • Goal: Identify the main idea of the entire paragraph.
  • • Trap: Distractor headings often contain exact words from the paragraph but only refer to a minor detail, not the main theme.
  • • Strategy: Read the first and last sentence of the paragraph carefully (topic sentences). Skim the middle for context.
Matching Features

"Match each statement with the correct researcher"

  • • Goal: Locate specific details and link them to categories (People, Dates, Locations).
  • • Trap: One person might have multiple statements, or some features might not be used at all.
  • • Strategy: Scan for the Capitalized Nouns (e.g., Scientist names) first. Underline them everywhere they appear. Then read around the name to match the statement.

3. Speaking Section (Part 2)

Instruction: Conquering the 2-Minute Monologue

The 1-Minute Prep: Instruct the student to write only keywords, not full sentences. They should use the bullet points on the cue card to structure their notes visually.

The P.P.F. Method (Extending Time): If the student runs out of things to say at 1 minute, teach them to shift tenses:

  • Past: "Before this happened, I used to..."
  • Present: "Nowadays, my opinion on this is..."
  • Future: "In the future, I plan to experience this again by..."

Topic Vocab
PROMPT 1

Describe an advertisement with a famous person in it.

Sample Structure Guide:
  • • What it is/Where: A billboard/TV commercial for a luxury watch or sports brand.
  • • Who the person is: A well-known athlete or actor. Briefly describe their public image.
  • • Why it's memorable: Catchy slogan, stunning visuals, or the contrast between the celebrity and the product.
PROMPT 2

Describe a piece of local news that people are interested in.

Sample Structure Guide:
  • • What the news was: The opening of a new public park, a major infrastructure project, or a local festival.
  • • Where you heard it: Social media feed, local broadcast, or word of mouth.
  • • Why people care: How it positively (or negatively) impacts the daily lives, traffic, or economy of the community.

Prompt 1

Famous Person Advertisement

"I’d like to talk about a really memorable advertisement I saw recently. It was a TV commercial for Rolex, the luxury watch brand, and it featured the legendary tennis player, Roger Federer. I actually saw it while I was watching a sports tournament on TV a few months ago, and it immediately caught my attention."

"In the ad, Federer isn't actually playing tennis. Instead, it shows clips of his greatest achievements, his elegant movements on the court, and moments where he shows great sportsmanship, all while a calm voiceover talks about the pursuit of perfection. He is, of course, wearing a classic Rolex watch."

"I think what makes this advertisement so effective is the perfect match between the celebrity and the product. Federer is known worldwide not just for his success, but for his class, endurance, and timeless style. These are the exact qualities that Rolex wants to associate with their watches."

"I found the ad really inspiring. It wasn't overly pushy or focused on selling the watch directly. Instead, it sold a concept—excellence and prestige. Even though I can't afford a Rolex right now, the ad definitely left a lasting impression on me, and I think it's a brilliant example of celebrity endorsement done right."

Prompt 2

Local News Item

"I’m going to describe a piece of local news that has really grabbed everyone's attention in my city recently. It’s about the transformation of a major road in the downtown area into a weekend pedestrian-only zone."

"I first heard about this on a local community Facebook group, and later it was all over the evening news on TV. The city council announced that starting next month, vehicles will be banned from this street every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday evening."

"People are extremely interested in this for a few reasons. First of all, our city is quite congested, and there’s a lack of open public spaces where people can just walk and relax safely without worrying about traffic. So, the idea of having a vibrant walking street with food stalls, street performers, and night markets is really exciting for locals."

"Secondly, a lot of small business owners are thrilled because it will definitely boost the local economy and attract more tourists to the area. Personally, I think it’s a fantastic initiative. It promotes a healthier lifestyle and gives the community a place to connect, and I’m already making plans to go there with my friends on the opening weekend."
