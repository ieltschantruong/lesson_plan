1. Reading Section

Reading Passage 3: Using Grammar Clues for Gap-Filling Tasks

In Passage 3, vocabulary can be highly specialized or abstract. Syntactical context and parts of speech clues are crucial to predicting and finding correct answers.

The Two Types of Gap-Filling
Type A: Words from Passage
  • Answers must be extracted **exactly** from the text.
  • Never change the tense, form, or spelling of the word.
  • CBT Hack: Double-click to highlight and drag to prevent typos.
Type B: Words from a List
  • The list of choices consists of heavily paraphrased synonyms.
  • You must identify the part of speech needed, then match synonyms.
  • Allows eliminating grammatically incorrect list options immediately.
Predictive Grammatical Signals

Prepositions: Blanks preceding/succeeding prepositions like "of", "in", "to", "for" usually require a Noun or a Gerund (-ing).

Articles & Quantifiers: Words like "a", "an", "the", "many", "each" indicate singular/plural countability rules.

Subject-Verb Agreement: Pay attention to plural subjects to instantly filter out singular verb forms in a word list.

2. Speaking Section

Speaking Part 3: Lexical Resources & Prompt Practice

Coping with Topical Gaps: When faced with an unfamiliar geography or abstract media question, train the student to use **paraphrastic strategies** and **lexical descriptors** rather than remaining silent.

Strategy Focus: Explaining Vocabulary when stuck

"I can't recall the specific technical word for it, but it basically refers to a phenomenon where..." or "In other words, it is a type of natural event that..."
