1. Listening Section (Part 2)

Introduction to Part 2 & Map Strategies (CBT Optimized)

Part 2 Overview: Usually a monologue set in an everyday social context (e.g., a speech about local facilities, a guide talking about a tour).

Computer-Based Map Strategies:

  • Find the Starting Point: Always locate the "You are here" mark, main entrance, or reception before the audio begins. This is your anchor.
  • Check the Compass/Layout: Look for a compass rose (N, S, E, W). If absent, mentally prepare to rely heavily on left/right and top/bottom positional language.
  • Cursor Tracking (CBT Essential): Since they cannot use a pen, advise the student to use their mouse cursor to physically trace the route on the screen in real-time. Eyes should stay locked on the screen, not looking down at the rough paper.
  • Know the CBT UI Interaction: Quickly check if the map requires drag-and-drop labels or selecting letters from a drop-down list. Knowing how to input the answer prevents clicking panic mid-audio.
  • Follow Question Order: The answers will ALWAYS appear in numerical order. If you lose track, look at the next number and wait for the speaker to arrive there.
  • Beware of Distractors: Speakers often mention a location but place something else there (e.g., "We originally planned to put the cafe here, but instead...").
  • Crucial Vocabulary: Drill prepositions of place like "adjacent to", "opposite", "at the junction", "dead end", "beyond", and "bend in the road".

2. Speaking Section (Part 1)

Instruction: IELTS Speaking Part 1

Format: A 4-5 minute face-to-face interview answering questions about familiar topics (Home, Family, Work, Studies, Interests).

Core Strategy: Expanding Answers

  • Do not give one-word answers ("Yes", "No").
  • Use the Answer + Reason + Detail/Example formula.
  • Keep it natural—it should sound like a conversation, not a memorized script.
