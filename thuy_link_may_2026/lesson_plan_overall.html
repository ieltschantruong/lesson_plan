⏱️ Lesson Breakdown (90-Minute Block)

00:00 - 00:30 (30m)
Speaking Diagnostic Mock

Full Test (Parts 1-3). Focus: Identifying 5.5 baseline gaps. Real-time audio recording and vocabulary analysis.

00:30 - 01:10 (40m)
Reading Match Logic

True/False/Not Given & Matching Headings. Breaking down logic: Why "Not Given" is not "False".

01:10 - 01:30 (20m)
Speed-Reading & Feedback

Scanning complex scientific or historical texts for keyword synonyms. Formulating direct corrective feedback map.

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Identify Synonyms in Passage: Band 6 candidates match words directly; Band 6.5 candidates look for conceptual paraphrases (e.g., "diminished size" instead of "smaller").
  • • Speaking Part 1 Expansion: Avoid short answers. Practice the "Answer + Reason + Example" extension format.

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Reading: Translating whole sentences in your head. It wastes precious minutes. Scan for structures instead of studying every adjective.
  • • Speaking: Pausing silently while thinking of precise academic vocabulary. Train to use filler phrases like, "That's an intriguing question..."
Assigned Homework
Timed Reading Practice Test (Full set - 3 Passages)

Practice under strict 60-minute conditions. Highlight all keywords and identify why mistakes were made using the review diary.

⏱️ Lesson Breakdown (90-Minute Block)

00:00 - 00:30 (30m)
Section 3 Distractors

Focus on multiple-choice traps. Learn to pinpoint speakers changing their minds (e.g., "Actually, let's go with the other project").

00:30 - 01:10 (40m)
Speaking Part 2 Structuring

The 1-Minute Brainstorm technique. Organizing descriptions chronologically or using narrative frameworks to speak effortlessly for 2 full minutes.

01:10 - 01:30 (20m)
Map & Diagram Navigation

Visual spatial prepositions practice. Pre-empting starting coordinates and directional indicators (e.g., "directly opposite", "northward deviation").

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Identify "But/However" Shifts: High-scoring students note that listening questions often present an incorrect option first, then correct it with an adversative conjunction.
  • • Speaking Part 2 Structure: Use a standard template framework to bridge across talking points smoothly (e.g., "Initially... moving onto the second aspect...").

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Listening: Writing an answer on the answer sheet before verifying if the speaker corrects themselves. Double-check the context clues.
  • • Listening: Incorrect pluralization endings or misspelled words. Note: Unnecessary spelling mistakes can cost you half a band score.
Assigned Homework
Listening Distractor & Map Homework

Complete 2 timed listening sections focusing on Section 3, then transcribe the exact sentences containing the answers.

⏱️ Lesson Breakdown (90-Minute Block)

00:00 - 00:45 (45m)
Writing Task 2 Structure

Introduction templates, body paragraph topic sentences, and generating convincing arguments. Focus on "Coherence and Cohesion."

00:45 - 01:15 (30m)
Speaking Part 3 (SEEL Method)

Structuring deep academic discussion answers using the SEEL template: State, Explain, Example, Link back.

01:15 - 01:30 (15m)
Writing Feedback Loop

Analyzing past writings line-by-line to replace simplistic vocabulary with complex Academic Word List alternatives.

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Learn Linkers Beyond "First", "Second": Implement dynamic cohesive transition devices such as "Furthermore," "Concurrently," "Consequently," and "By contrast."
  • • Clear Main Idea: Ensure each paragraph represents one main argument that directly references the prompt.

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Writing: Writing without taking 5 minutes to outline. This leads to unfocused essays, lowering your "Coherence and Cohesion" score to a 5.0.
  • • Writing: Relying on repetitive verb structures. Vary your syntax with passive structures and relative clauses.
Assigned Homework
Writing Brainstorm Homework & Speaking Cue Cards

Write full outlines for 3 Writing Task 2 topics. Record yourself answering 3 Part-2 Cue Cards (2 minutes each).

⏱️ Lesson Breakdown (90-Minute Block)

00:00 - 00:25 (25m)
Full Speaking Mock 1

Testing live prompts. Evaluation focus: Grammatical range and continuous speaking capability without hesitating.

00:25 - 01:05 (40m)
Target Error Corrections

Detailed review of pronunciation hurdles and grammatical gaps (e.g., subject-verb agreement or incorrect tenses).

01:05 - 01:30 (25m)
Full Speaking Mock 2

High-level test simulation featuring abstract, difficult themes. Focus: Smoothly incorporating academic lexical chunks.

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Replace Vague Terms: Swap out simple words like "good", "bad", "happy", "problem" with "beneficial", "detrimental", "enthusiastic", "obstacle".
  • • Master Self-Correction: Instantly correct errors smoothly without stalling the flow of your speech.

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Speaking: Sounding robotic. Try to speak naturally. Avoid memorizing full responses because the examiner will recognize this instantly and lower your score.
  • • Speaking: Drifting too far from the cue card prompts. Make sure to touch upon every point listed on the card.
Assigned Homework
Speaking Self-Evaluation Challenge

Record answers for 3 different cue card themes. Listen to your recordings and write down any repetitive vocabulary you notice.

⏱️ Lesson Breakdown (90-Minute Block)

00:00 - 00:30 (30m)
Reading & Listening Speedrun

Tackling difficult sections. Finding synonyms and completing sentence-gaps at an accelerated pace.

00:30 - 01:00 (30m)
Writing Structure Polishing

Perfecting visual trend descriptions for Task 1, as well as complex supporting arguments for Task 2.

01:00 - 01:30 (30m)
Speaking Part 3 Challenge

Answering tough, unfamiliar questions. Focus on thinking on your feet and using logical transition structures.

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Master Time Allocation: Make sure you never spend more than 20 minutes on Passage 1 or Writing Task 1.
  • • Identify High-Frequency Topics: Practice key vocabulary clusters for common exam topics like Technology, Environment, Education, and Health.

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Consolidation: Don't try to learn entirely new grammar structures this close to the exam. Instead, focus on perfecting the ones you already know.
  • • Time Management: Never skip writing introductions or conclusions in practice, as missing sections will instantly drop your Task Achievement score to a 5.0.

00:00 - 01:00 (60m)
Full Simulated Writing Mock

Completing both Task 1 and Task 2 under timed exam conditions. Focus on writing legibility, vocabulary choice, and self-editing.

01:00 - 01:15 (15m)
Simulated Speaking Exam

A formal mock speaking test. Feedback will be provided on pronunciation, grammar range, and fluency.

01:15 - 01:30 (15m)
Final Analysis & Action Plan

Detailed analysis of your progress, identifying remaining weak spots, and outlining your final strategy for exam day.

5.5-6.0 → 6.5 Core Transition Tactics

  • • Master Self-Monitoring: During the exam, keep a mental checklist of your common errors to catch and correct spelling or subject-verb issues.
  • • Manage Your Energy: Build stamina to stay focused and alert during the intense 3-hour exam.

Common Pitfalls (Stop Doing This)

  • • Exam Day: Writing too close to the word limit (aim for 160-180 words for Task 1, 260-280 words for Task 2 to ensure you develop your ideas thoroughly).
  • • Exam Day: Spending too long on a single reading question. If you are stuck, guess the answer and move on.
