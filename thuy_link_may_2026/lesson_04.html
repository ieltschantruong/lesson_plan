Open Strategy Sheet

Avoid "I Think" Loops

To hit 6.5, replace repetitive personal opinions with structural, objective observations (e.g., "From my perspective...", "It is widely perceived that...", "Predominantly...").

Self-Correction Control

Errors occur under pressure. Do not spiral or repeat words multiple times. Smoothly rephrase the sentence once using professional transitions (e.g., "Or rather...", "What I meant to say is...").

Structure Complex Themes

Structure Part 3 answers using the **SEEL Method**: **S**tate the main argument clearly, **E**xplain your logic, provide a concrete **E**xample, and **L**ink back to the question.

Part 2 Cue Card Guide:

Describe a movie you enjoyed watching.
• Where and when you saw it
• Who you saw the movie with
• What the movie was about
• And explain why you enjoyed watching this movie

Part 3 Direct Discussion Prompts:
  • In your country, what kinds of movies do people like to watch?
  • Do people like to watch movies at home or in the cinema?
  • Why don't some people enjoy watching movies?
  • What qualities do people need to be good actors?
  • Which is more important for an actor, acting skills or appearance?
  • Do you agree or disagree that being an actor is a difficult job?
  • How important are famous actors to the success of a movie?
Comparative Case Analysis:

Band 5.5 - 6.0 Level (Simple & Repetitive):

"I want to tell you about a movie called Interstellar. I went to the cinema with my best friend last year to see it. It is a science-fiction movie about a group of astronauts who go to space to find a new planet for humans to live. I liked this movie because the story is very exciting and the graphics look so real. It was a good movie and made me happy."

Band 6.5+ Level (Cohesive, Idiomatic, Precise):

"I’d like to speak about a film that left an indelible impression on me, which is Interstellar. I watched this cinematic masterpiece An outstanding movie of high artistic value. at a premium theater in Da Nang roughly a year ago, accompanied by my classmate. The movie delivers a captivating narrative A highly interesting, attention-grabbing story. centering on an audacious group of space explorers searching for a habitable planet to secure human survival.

I was absolutely mesmerized by its visual effects, which were paired with an emotionally resonant Evoking deep emotional connections and feelings. musical score. Ultimately, I thoroughly enjoyed the film because it wasn’t merely passive entertainment; rather, it explored a thought-provoking theme An idea that inspires deep logical thinking. regarding the preservation of human consciousness and love crossing spatial dimensions, elevated by stellar performances Exceptional acting and delivery by movie stars. from the cast."

Part 2 Cue Card Guide:

Describe a car, motorbike or bicycle trip that you think would be interesting.
• Where you want to go
• Who you want to go with
• How easy or difficult the trip would be
• And explain why you think using a car, motorbike or bicycle for this trip would be interesting

Part 3 Direct Discussion Prompts:
  • Are bicycles and motorbikes popular in your country?
  • Which form of vehicle is more popular in your country, bikes, cars or motorcycles?
  • What is the best thing about public transport in the area you live?
  • Do you agree that more and more people want to buy cars these days?
  • How do people travel today in cities and in the countryside?
  • Do city planners consider the needs of pedestrians enough?
  • What impact can an inadequate transport or transportation system have on a country?
Comparative Case Analysis:

Band 5.5 - 6.0 Level:

"I want to go on a motorcycle trip from Da Nang to Hue. I will go with my friends on a weekend. The trip is not very difficult because the road is big, but going over the Hai Van pass can be dangerous if it rains. Riding a motorcycle is great because you can see beautiful mountains and beaches easily and stop to take pictures."

Band 6.5+ Level (Cohesive, Idiomatic, Precise):

"I’ve long contemplated embarking on a motorbike journey along the rugged coastal highways of central Vietnam, specifically from Da Nang to Hue. I plan to organize this exhilarating journey A highly exciting and thrilling travel experience. with my close companions who share my passion for travel. We prefer taking an unconventional route A route that is not commonly taken or touristy. crossing over the iconic pass to witness the scenic landscapes Beautiful, sweeping natural views of the countryside. firsthand.

Admittedly, navigating the steep hairpin turns could turn into an arduous trek A physically demanding and challenging journey. especially during inclement weather. Nonetheless, traveling on two wheels is uniquely interesting as it grants an unparalleled sense of autonomy A feeling of total independence and self-direction. allowing us to pause and absorb the atmosphere in a way that is simply impossible within the confines of a tour bus."
