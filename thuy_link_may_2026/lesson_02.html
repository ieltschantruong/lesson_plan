Question Guide Launch Drill Test 3

1. Distractor Detection

To breach 6.5, watch out for speakers correcting their spelling, address details, or opinions (e.g., "Actually, let's make that Tuesday instead"). Never write down the first answer you hear without analyzing the immediate context.

2. Spatial Map Pre-Emption

During the 30-second reading time, trace starting junctions, compass symbols, and left/right indicators. Highlight terms like "opposite", "adjacent to", "corridor", or "bend of the road".

3. Grammatical Prediction

Analyze blanks before listening. Determine if you require an adjective, a plural noun, or a numeric code. If the text says "a variety of ____", prepare to catch a plural noun.

Part 2 Cue Card Prompt Smile & Sentiment Domain

Candidate Prompt Directive:

Describe an occasion you remember when you saw a lot of people smiling.

  • Where you were and when it happened
  • Who you were with
  • What happened
  • And explain why you remember this occasion when people smiled

"I remember a day when I saw many people smiling. It was my cousin's wedding last year. I was there with my family. The wedding was big. We ate cake and we took photos. Everyone was smiling because they were happy for the couple. I remember this because it was very fun and we got many good memories together."

"I’d like to share an occasion that is vividly etchedClearly remembered or permanently embedded in memory. in my mind. It happened last autumn during my cousin's wedding ceremony in Hanoi. I was surrounded byEncircled or accompanied by. my entire extended family, and the atmosphere was absolutely electricExtremely exciting, thrilling, or vibrant..

What truly stood out was when the newlyweds stepped onto the stage and gave an unexpected, lighthearted dance performance. It was a completely spontaneousUnplanned, voluntary, or natural. act, and the guests immediately erupted into unbounded laughterLimitless or uncontrollable happy laughing.. Seeing a diverse crowd of older and younger relatives beaming with sheer delightPure happiness or intense joy. was a profoundly heartwarmingUplifting, tender, and emotionally satisfying. sight.

I remember this distinct event so clearly because it highlights the infectious natureThe quality of easily spreading to other people. of joy. In a fast-paced world where everyone is perpetually busy, witnessing a group of people momentarily letting go of their stressors to unite in smiles was a rare, beautiful phenomenon."

Part 3 Abstract Strategy Prompts

1. Why do people smile?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Predominantly, people utilize smiles as a social tool to signal amenityFriendliness, pleasantness, or a polite social disposition. and build rapid rapport. On a deeper physiological level, a smile is a natural reflex to pleasurable stimuli, triggering endorphins that instantly alleviate psychological pressure."

2. Do children tend to smile more frequently than adults?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Without a doubt, children do. This stems from their absence of cognitive loadThe mental burden associated with stress, duties, or social pressures. and social expectations. Adults are constantly preoccupied with occupational or financial issues, which naturally reduces spontaneous displays of joy."

3. Why do people usually smile when taking photos?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"This is largely due to societal conventions and a desire to project an ideal imageTo display a perfect, happy, and successful version of oneself to society. of themselves. Photographs are meant to capture milestones, and smiling ensures those memories are framed in positivity, even if it feels manufactured."

4. Do older people have better control over their feelings compared to young people?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"In general, yes. Senior citizens have cultivated emotional resilienceThe capability to adapt smoothly to stressors and regulate emotional outbursts. over decades of life experience. Young people are often driven by turbulent hormones and impulses, making them highly reactive to environmental triggers."

5. When is it important for people not to show their emotions?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"There are critical corporate or political scenarios where displaying emotions can compromise professional negotiation. For instance, in diplomacy or board meetings, maintaining a neutral facadeA flat or neutral expression that reveals no personal thoughts or emotions. is vital to preserve authority."

6 & 7. Are people motivated more by their emotions or by logical thinking? Is this drive beneficial?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Despite our belief in our logical capacity, human action is profoundly underpinned by emotionMotivated, supported, or driven fundamentally by underlying feelings.. While rational logic dictates safe choices, emotion inspires passion. However, being blindly driven by feeling can be highly detrimentalHarmful, damaging, or counterproductive. in financial or structural situations where calm calculations are necessary."

Part 2 Cue Card Prompt Public Figure Domain

Candidate Prompt Directive:

Describe a famous person who you would like to meet.

  • Who he/she is and what he/she does
  • What he/she is famous for
  • How long this person has been famous
  • And explain why you would like to meet this famous person
Show Comparative Answers & Band Breakdown (Part 2)

Band 5.5 - 6.0 Level (Simple Structure):

"I want to meet a famous singer called Taylor Swift. She is famous because she writes great songs and sings them beautifully. She has been famous for a long time, maybe fifteen years. I would like to meet her because she seems friendly and I want to ask her how she writes her lyrics and songs."

Band 6.5+ Level (Precise, Academic, Grammatically Diverse):

"I’ve long held an aspiration to meet Keanu Reeves, an esteemedHighly respected and admired. Canadian actor renowned for his roles in blockbusters like *The Matrix* and *John Wick*. He has occupied a spot in the limelightThe focus of public attention, fame, or notoriety. for over three decades, continuously evolving as a versatile performer.

What truly distinguishes him is not just his cinematic credentialsQualifications, achievements, or training in a career field., but his remarkably quiet and philanthropic lifestyleA generous life dedicated to donating money and helping others.. Unlike many contemporary celebrities who actively court sensationalism, Reeves resides in quiet humility and donates massive portions of his earnings to cancer research without seeking personal publicity.

I would jump at the chance to converse with him to understand how he maintains such absolute equanimityMental calmness, composure, and emotional balance under pressure. amidst the chaotic, superficial environment of Hollywood. He represents the ideal paradigm of balancing immense public success with authentic personal character."

Part 3 Abstract Strategy Prompts

1. What kinds of famous people are often in the news in your country?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Predominantly, mainstream entertainment artists, athletic heroes, and entrepreneurial figures dominate national coverage. In Vietnam, football players and social media influencers attract a disproportionate amountAn unequal, massive, or unfairly large share. of media attention because their lives feed the viral requirements of online channels."

2. Why do children want to become famous?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Young youngsters often associate celebrity status with unrivaled adorationAn extremely high, unconditional level of love and respect from public crowds. and material affluence. They look at content platforms where creators seem to live exciting lifestyles, making them completely oblivious to the isolation and loss of privacy behind the scenes."

3. Are there more celebrities in the news now compared to the past?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Undeniably. The democratization of broadcasting through internet avenues has led to a proliferation of micro-celebritiesA rapid, massive increase of public figures famous inside tiny, specialized niches.. In the past, fame was guarded by elite networks; today, viral algorithms can elevate a regular citizen overnight."

4. Do you agree that everyone enjoys reading news about celebrities?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Actually, I would argue that is a misconception. While high-volume tabloid clicks suggest universal interest, a significant demographic finds celebrity culture highly banalExtremely boring, predictable, and devoid of original substance. and actively avoids it, opting instead for socio-economic reporting."

5 & 6. Do people like packaged celebrities or those with genuine talent? Can fame exist without talent?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Initially, manufactured personalities create dramatic viral impact, but their longevity is exceptionally brief. Audiences eventually gravitate towards individuals possessing virtuositySuperior skill, exceptional talent, or technical mastery in a particular domain.. While fame can temporarily be generated without talent through publicity stunts, sustaining it demands genuine creative capital."

7. Would people still want to become famous if they knew what they are going to face?

View Model Answer Breakdown

"Many would still aggressively chase it because human vanity and ambition often overshadow reasonTo dominate or make logical thinking secondary to overwhelming impulse.. Despite knowing the psychological tax, the pursuit of legacy and social supremacy remains an extremely potent motivator."
