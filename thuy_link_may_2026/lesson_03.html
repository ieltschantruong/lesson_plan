Task 1 Comparative Strategy (Pie Charts)

  • • Draft an accurate Overview: Never include numbers in your overview. State the overall visual trend (e.g., "North America and Africa collectively hold the majority of... while timber production is concentrated in...").
  • • Group logically: Group regions with high parameters into Body Paragraph 1 and lesser percentages into Body Paragraph 2.
  • • Use precise comparison structures: Practice phrases like "dramatically outnumbers", "stands in stark contrast to", and "represents the smallest fraction of".

Task 2 Discussion & Opinion Strategy

  • • Outline balanced paragraphs: Body 1 must outline horizontal city structures; Body 2 must discuss vertical housing; and your own opinion must be consistently integrated.
  • • Maintain clear topic sentences: Every body paragraph must start with a distinct, single argument statement (no messy multiple thoughts).
  • • Emphasize Task Achievement (6.5 target): Avoid generalizations. Back up your points with concrete scenarios (e.g., "In highly-populated hubs like Tokyo... vertical living optimization is vital").

Task 1: World's Forest & Timber Pie Charts

Key Features of the Diagram:

This task requires selecting and reporting the main features of world forest distribution and timber volume across five main regions (Africa, North America, South America, Asia, and Europe).

Comparison Points to Capture:
  • Dominance: North America and South America collectively contain over half of the global timber distribution, while holding a smaller ratio of the actual forest area.
  • Discrepancies: Africa represents a significant portion of world forest area but a minor proportion of timber volume.
  • Balance: Europe's proportion of forest and timber volume remains relatively proportional.
Task 2 Debate: Vertical vs Horizontal Cities

Prompt: Some people think that it is best to live in a horizontal city while others think of a vertical city. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

View A: Horizontal Living (Low-rise, Sprawling)

  • Pros: Spacious gardens, sense of neighborhood community, safer from structural disasters, lower cost of initial family homes.
  • Cons: Severe urban sprawl, deforestation of surrounding landscapes, extreme commuter distances, increased greenhouse emissions.

View B: Vertical Living (High-rise, Dense)

  • Pros: Extremely land-efficient, shorter commutes, unified municipal services, preserves rural lands from structural destruction.
  • Cons: Overcrowding, high maintenance fees, risk of social isolation, highly dependent on modern elevators and continuous electrical grids.
