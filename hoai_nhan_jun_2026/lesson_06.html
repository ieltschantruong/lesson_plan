Pedagogical Framework

Speaking Part 2 Strategic Overview

Understand the mechanics behind breaking the 2-minute speaking plateau.

Bullet-point Keyword Mapping

Discard complete sentence scripting. In 60 seconds, write down exactly **4-5 vertical core keyword triggers** coupled with strong, sensory adjectives. This keeps the eyes scanning rather than reading.

The PPF Timeline Trick

Unpack her answers along a Past-Present-Future narrative skeleton. Spending 40s on context/past details, 50s on present status, and 30s on hypothetical future outcomes easily fills the required 2 minutes.

4-3-2 Fluency Repetition

Speaking on the same topic for 4 minutes, then compressing it to 3, and finally to 2. It forces the cognitive system to discard syntactic processing pauses, making the 2-minute run incredibly dense and fluent.

Technology & Media Transition
Test 11 Future Tech

Tech to Own in Future

"Describe a piece of technology you would like to own in the future."

  • What it is and how you knew about it
  • What you would use it for
  • Explain why you would like to own it
Lexical Targets: Cutting-edge device, obsolete setup, automated pipelines, revolutionize, streamline, paradigm shift.
Test 13 Media Show

TV or Online Programme

"Describe a TV or online programme that you enjoy watching."

  • What it is about and how often you watch it
  • Which country it comes from
  • Explain why you enjoy watching this programme
Lexical Targets: Episodic commentary, binge-watch, documentary style, thought-provoking narrative, captivating, informative.
Test 28 Tech Glitch

Encountered Tech Problem

"Describe a problem of technology you have encountered, like a computer or a phone."

  • What it was, when and where you had it
  • How the problem was solved
  • Explain how you felt when you had it
Lexical Targets: System outage, software bug, debug on-the-fly, exasperated, troubleshoot, resolution, seamless recovery.
