01 Describe an occasion when you lost your way

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where you were going; Who you were with; How you lost your way; And explain how you felt and handled the situation.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I’d like to share an occasion when I became completely disoriented while trekking in the lush mountain valleys surrounding Sapa with my cousin. We had planned to hike to a secluded waterfall based on hand-drawn maps we received at our homestay. However, as deep mountain fog swept in rapidly, we missed a critical fork in the trail and found ourselves essentially in the middle of nowhere. Since there was no cellular coverage whatsoever, our digital maps were useless, and my heart sank immediately. We had to keep our heads, so we decided to retrace our steps calmly and follow a small stream downhill. Fortunately, we crossed paths with a local farmer who kindly pointed us back toward the main trail. It was a nerve-wracking experience, but it taught me an invaluable lesson about preparing alternative navigational backups."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Disoriented (adjective) /dɪsˈɔː.ri.en.tɪd/

Definition: Confused and not knowing where you are or which direction you are facing.
Collocations: Become disoriented, completely disoriented.
Example: The dense mist on the mountain passes caused us to become disoriented within minutes.

2. Navigational (adjective) /ˌnæv.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən.əl/

Definition: Relating to finding the way to a destination.
Collocations: Navigational backup, navigational skills.
Example: In remote regions, carrying offline navigational tools is an absolute necessity.

3. Get one's bearings (idiom)

Definition: To find out exactly where you are or what you should do next.
Example: We paused at the top of the ridge to look at the landscape and try to get our bearings.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How has the widespread usage of GPS and smartphone navigation changed human cognitive mapping?
Ans (OREO): In my view, relying heavily on automated GPS tools has significantly weakened our organic navigational skills. This is because users passively follow turn-by-turn voice directions instead of actively processing landmark signals and spatial layouts. For instance, many drivers fail to remember basic routes they traverse daily without digital assistance. Therefore, while modern technology offers incredible convenience, it has diluted our natural ability to map environments mentally.

02 Describe an outdoor sport you would like to try for the first time

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What sport it is; Where you can do it; What equipment is needed; And explain why you would like to try this outdoor sport.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"An outdoor sport that I have been deeply yearning to try is windsurfing, which is a fantastic combination of sailing and surfing. I would love to try this at the coastal town of Mui Ne, which is widely celebrated for its consistent, strong winds and excellent wave conditions. The necessary equipment consists of a specialized board, a rig containing a mast, boom, and sail, alongside safety gear like a life vest and a wetsuit. I want to try this sport because it seems like an incredibly exhilarating way to harness the raw momentum of both wind and water. While I know it has a notoriously steep learning curve and demands robust core strength, I am eager to break out of my comfort zone, dive headfirst into this challenge, and experience the sheer thrill of gliding across the ocean waves."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Exhilarating (adjective) /ɪɡˈzɪl.ə.reɪ.tɪŋ/

Definition: Making you feel extremely happy, excited, and full of energy.
Collocations: Exhilarating experience, exhilarating ride.
Example: Gliding across open water at high speeds is an exhilarating sensation like no other.

2. Momentum (noun) /məˈmen.təm/

Definition: The force or speed with which something moves.
Collocations: Gain momentum, gather momentum.
Example: The sail caught a sudden gust, helping the board gather momentum across the water.

3. Dive headfirst into (idiom)

Definition: To start doing something with immense enthusiasm and energy without hesitation.
Example: Instead of worrying about falling, I decided to dive headfirst into my first windsurfing lesson.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why are modern metropolitan youths showing a growing preference for adventurous outdoor sports over traditional team sports?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, the shift toward extreme outdoor sports is a direct reaction to the highly structured, sedentary nature of urban lives. Adventurous sports like windsurfing or rock climbing offer a pure, sensory adrenaline rush that traditional sports like soccer cannot replicate, alongside a profound escape into nature. For example, a young corporate professional gains deep therapeutic release from navigating open waves. Therefore, these sports serve as active, psychological outlets to conquer fears and experience raw freedom.

03 Describe an unusual but interesting building you would like to visit

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where this building is; What it looks like; How you first learned about it; And explain why you would like to visit this unusual building.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"An unusual but completely fascinating landmark that I am highly eager to visit is the famous 'Crazy House' (officially named Hang Nga Guesthouse) located in the mountainous city of Da Lat. This extraordinary architectural masterpiece resembles a giant, organic banyan tree, featuring twisted, cave-like stone pathways, spiderweb windows, and animal-themed guestrooms built directly into its structure. I first stumbled upon this building in an online art magazine highlighting avant-garde structures. I want to visit this place because it completely breaks the mold of traditional, rigid urban planning. It is a stunning, physical manifestation of artistic expression, showing a beautiful juxtaposition between man-made structures and the chaotic, majestic forms of raw nature. Standing inside its twisted corridors would be an absolute feast for the eyes."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Avant-garde (adjective) /ˌæv.ɒ̃ˈɡɑːd/

Definition: Ultra-modern, highly innovative, and experimenting with new style concepts.
Example: The architect's avant-garde approach to using recycled plastics has won global acclaim.

2. Architectural (adjective) /ˌɑː.kɪˈtek.tʃər.əl/

Definition: Relating to the design and construction of buildings.
Collocations: Architectural masterpiece, architectural styles.
Example: The historic cathedral is celebrated for its unique Goth-style architectural features.

3. Break the mold (idiom)

Definition: To do something completely different from what has been done before, rejecting conventional limits.
Example: Designing a house that looks like a giant tree really broke the mold of standard modern housing.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Should municipal governments invest heavily in preserving historic buildings, even if the maintenance costs are exceptionally high?
Ans (OREO): In my view, safeguarding historical architecture is paramount for maintaining a city's cultural identity. This is because historic buildings serve as a tangible link to a community’s past struggles, aesthetic achievements, and historical narrative, which modern glass skyscrapers cannot replicate. For example, preserving traditional wooden townhouses in Hoi An sustains the city's historical soul and local tourist economy. Therefore, these landmarks prevent cultural erosion and deserve public funding despite high maintenance costs.

04 Describe a person who helps to protect the environment

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Who this person is; How you know him/her; What they do to protect the environment; And explain why you admire this person.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I would love to tell you about an exceptional, eco-conscious classmate named Lan, who is incredibly active in green initiatives. We met during our freshman orientation, and we've collaborated on multiple school projects since. Lan runs a highly successful community-driven zero-waste advocacy campaign in our neighborhood. Every single weekend, she organizes a group of student volunteers to clean up local riverside parks and sort recyclable plastics. Furthermore, she conducts informative workshops at primary schools, teaching young kids the importance of reducing single-use packaging. I deeply admire Lan because she truly leads by example; she lives a completely plastic-free lifestyle, carrying her own reusable containers everywhere. Her absolute devotion and infectious energy prove that any single person, with enough passion, can make a monumental difference in protecting our local ecosystems."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Eco-conscious (adjective) /ˌiː.kəʊˈkɒn.ʃəs/

Definition: Showing deep concern for the preservation of the environment and minimizing pollution.
Collocations: Eco-conscious consumer, eco-conscious lifestyle.
Example: Purchasing locally sourced items is a key habit of eco-conscious citizens.

2. Advocacy (noun) /ˈæd.və.kə.si/

Definition: Public support for or recommendation of a particular cause or policy.
Collocations: Environmental advocacy, legal advocacy.
Example: Her non-profit organization is dedicated to clean water advocacy in remote delta communities.

3. Lead by example (idiom)

Definition: To guide or influence others through your own personal, positive actions instead of words.
Example: Lan always leads by example, showing up first at the weekend community cleanups.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: What is the most effective way for public educational institutions to instill active environmental values in children?
Ans (OREO): In my view, integrating hands-on ecological projects directly into the primary school curriculum is highly effective. This is because theoretical textbook lectures fail to foster actual empathy, whereas real experiences build a lifetime bond with nature. For example, schools managing small organic gardens teach children about biodiversity and food waste through daily care. Therefore, physical engagement develops actual eco-conscious citizens far better than classroom exams.

05 Describe an important old thing that your family has kept for a long time

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What this thing is; How your family got it; How long your family has kept it; And explain why this old thing is so important to your family.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I want to share the story of a precious mechanical Swiss watch inherited from my great-grandfather, which is an invaluable family heirloom. This beautiful watch was originally purchased in the late 1940s, and it has been preserved by our family for almost eighty years. It features an elegant silver dial, a worn brown leather strap, and a visible mechanical movement that still winds perfectly. What makes this old watch so important to us is its immense sentimental value. It has accompanied our family through thick and thin, surviving various socio-economic transitions and wars. To me, it is a brilliant symbol of historical resilience and family continuity. Staring at its rhythmic hands ticking smoothly is a grounding reminder of my ancestors' struggles and successes, proving that some objects possess a timeless soul that digital replacements can never replicate."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Heirloom (noun) /ˈeə.luːm/

Definition: A valuable object that has belonged to a family for several generations.
Collocations: Family heirloom, cherished heirloom.
Example: Her mother gave her a vintage jade necklace as a treasured family heirloom.

2. Sentimental (adjective) /ˌsen.tɪˈmen.təl/

Definition: Relating to feelings of tenderness, nostalgia, or sadness.
Collocations: Sentimental value, sentimental attachment.
Example: Although the old wooden desk was practically worthless, it held deep sentimental value for my father.

3. Through thick and thin (idiom)

Definition: Under all circumstances, surviving both the good times and very difficult challenges.
Example: My parents stood by each other through thick and thin for over forty years.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why do some cultures place a significantly higher value on preserving antique objects than others?
Ans (OREO): In my view, cultures that emphasize ancestral worship and family legacy naturally prioritize the preservation of antiques. This is because ancient objects are viewed as sacred conduits of historical continuity, grounding current generations in their ancestral origins. For instance, in many traditional East Asian households, families maintain shrines featuring historic tools and heirlooms to pay respect. Therefore, keeping these items is a vital moral duty that preserves collective memory and resists modern individualistic consumerism.

06 Describe a natural talent (sports, music, etc.) you want to improve

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What this natural talent is; How you discovered you had it; How you want to improve it; And explain why you want to improve this natural talent.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A natural talent that I have always wanted to systematically improve is playing the piano by ear. I discovered this gift during childhood, when I realized I could replicate simple melodies on a toy keyboard without any formal sheet music. While I am not a child prodigy by any means, I possess an intuitive sense of pitch and melody. Currently, I want to elevate this skill to absolute mastery by studying advanced jazz improvisation and music theory under a certified tutor. I want to improve this talent because music is my ultimate psychological sanctuary; it allows me to translate my raw emotions into complex, beautiful harmonies. Enhancing this skill would not only boost my personal confidence but also let me connect on a much deeper level with listeners, playing moving songs that truly strike a chord."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Prodigy (noun) /ˈprɒd.ɪ.dʒi/

Definition: A young person who has an exceptional natural ability or talent for something.
Collocations: Child prodigy, musical prodigy.
Example: At only six, the child prodigy played complex Bach sonatas flawlessly.

2. Mastery (noun) /ˈmɑː.stər.i/

Definition: Complete control, deep understanding, or elite skill in a particular subject or activity.
Collocations: Absolute mastery, gain mastery over.
Example: Achieving absolute mastery of the violin requires thousands of hours of deliberate practice.

3. Strike a chord (idiom)

Definition: To create a strong, emotional response or connect deeply with others.
Example: Her moving performance of the folk song struck a chord with the older audience.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Do you believe natural talent is more important than hard work (deliberate practice) in achieving elite mastery in creative fields?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, deliberate practice and hard work far outweigh raw, natural talent. This is because talent only provides an initial, baseline advantage, whereas continuous, structured effort is what develops the actual skill into elite mastery. For example, a child born with perfect pitch must still study music theory and practice scale exercises for decades to become an actual concert pianist. Therefore, as the proverb goes, practice makes perfect, and grit is the key driver of long-term success.
