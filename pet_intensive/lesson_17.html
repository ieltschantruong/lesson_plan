01 Describe a foreign country you would like to visit in the future

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Which country it is; How you first learned about it; What you would like to do there; And explain why you would like to visit this country in the future.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A foreign country that has captured my imagination, and which I am determined to visit in the near future, is Japan. I first learned about Japan through travel documentaries on National Geographic when I was a teenager, and since then, my interest has grown exponentially. If I get the opportunity to travel there, I would love to explore the juxtaposition of its hyper-modern metropolises and its tranquil, revered historic sites. I want to experience the absolute hustle and bustle of Tokyo, but also escape to Kyoto's serene temples during the cherry blossom season. The primary reason for my desire to visit is the exquisite blend of cutting-edge technology and ancient traditions, which is beautifully preserved in Japanese culture. Additionally, I am an avid fan of culinary exploration, so embarking on a street-food tour to sample authentic sushi and ramen directly from local artisans is high on my bucket list. I believe this journey would offer a profound level of cultural immersion that would widen my horizons."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Juxtaposition (noun) /ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃn/

Definition: The fact of putting two things close together with contrasting effect.
Collocations: A striking juxtaposition, juxtaposition of old and new.
Example: The city's landscape presents an interesting juxtaposition of ancient temples and modern skyscrapers.

2. Metropolis (noun) /məˈtrɒpəlɪs/

Definition: A very large, busy, and important city, often the capital of a country or region.
Collocations: Bustling metropolis, sprawling metropolis.
Example: Tokyo is a massive metropolis that offers endless opportunities for exploration.

3. Cultural immersion (noun phrase)

Definition: Fully integrating oneself into a foreign culture to understand its customs, language, and values deeply.
Example: Staying with a local family in Kyoto provided an unmatched cultural immersion.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How has international tourism impacted local cultures in developing countries?
Ans (OREO): From my perspective, international tourism has had a double-edged impact on local cultures. On the one hand, it raises public awareness and generates crucial funds to preserve historical landmarks and indigenous crafts. For example, tourism in Hue has directly supported the restoration of its imperial Citadel. On the other hand, commercialization often leads to the dilution of authentic traditions, as local festivals are tailored to meet foreign tastes. Therefore, while tourism brings economic vitality, careful regulations are necessary to protect cultural authenticity.

02 Describe a city that you have been to and would like to visit again

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What city it is; When you went there; What you did during your visit; And explain why you would like to visit this city again.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A city that I have been to and absolutely yearn to visit again is Da Lat, a picturesque highland city in the southern parts of Vietnam. I went there about two years ago with my family to escape the sweltering heat of the summer season. During our stay, we did a variety of relaxing activities, such as renting a pedal boat on Xuan Huong Lake, wandering through the local night market, and visiting several colonial French villas hidden deep within the pine woods. What makes this city so alluring is its ethereal misty weather and tranquil atmosphere. It feels like an entirely different world compared to the noisy, crowded urban spaces. I would jump at the opportunity to visit Da Lat again because the slow pace of life and fresh mountain air acted as a highly therapeutic escape for me. It is the perfect place to decompress and recharge my batteries after a long semester of academic pressure."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Picturesque (adjective) /ˌpɪktʃəˈresk/

Definition: Visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way.
Collocations: Picturesque village, picturesque scenery.
Example: Da Lat is famous for its picturesque valleys and pine forests.

2. Ethereal (adjective) /iˈθɪəriəl/

Definition: Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems not to be of this world; heavenly.
Example: The early morning mist covering Xuan Huong Lake gave the city an ethereal beauty.

3. Escape the hustle and bustle (idiom)

Definition: To move away from a busy, noisy, and crowded urban area to a peaceful location.
Example: Many city dwellers visit Da Lat on weekends to escape the hustle and bustle of Saigon.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: What urban planning factors make a city highly liveable for its residents?
Ans (OREO): In my view, green spaces and reliable public transportation are the key pillars of a liveable city. This is because ample parks improve physical and mental health, while efficient transit lines minimize commuting stress and air pollution. For instance, cities like Singapore integrate vertical gardens and extensive subway networks directly into their residential zones, making daily routines highly seamless. Therefore, a focus on ecological sustainability and accessibility is essential to elevate the overall quality of urban life.

03 Describe your favorite place in your home where you like to relax

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where this place is; What it looks like; What you do there to relax; And explain why this is your favorite place to relax in your home.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"My absolute favorite place in my home where I go to wind down is the small balcony connected to my bedroom. It's a cozy, secluded space that looks out onto our quiet backyard garden. Over the last couple of years, I’ve decorated it with several potted herbs, hanging ivy, and a soft, comfortable wooden armchair. Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I sit out there with a hot cup of tea and a good novel. Sometimes, I simply put on my headphones, play some ambient music, and listen to the birds. This balcony acts as a highly personal sanctuary where I can decompress and shut out the noise of the outside world. I love it because it’s quiet, private, and filled with natural sunlight. It offers an invaluable environment for quiet contemplation, making it the perfect spot to find my peace of mind."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Sanctuary (noun) /ˈsæŋktʃuəri/

Definition: A place of safety, refuge, or holiness, where one can find peace from danger or difficulty.
Collocations: Private sanctuary, safe sanctuary.
Example: After a hectic day at work, my library room becomes my private sanctuary.

2. Decompress (verb) /ˌdiːkəmˈpres/

Definition: To relax and release tension or pressure after a period of hard work or stress.
Collocations: Decompress after work, decompress mentally.
Example: Taking a hot shower and listening to classical music helps me decompress quickly.

3. Secluded (adjective) /sɪˈkluːdɪd/

Definition: Sheltered, quiet, and private; far away from other people or busy activity.
Collocations: Secluded spot, secluded balcony.
Example: We found a secluded beach where we could read our books without interruption.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Is it important for individuals to have a designated personal space at home?
Ans (OREO): In my view, having a designated personal space is absolutely essential for an individual's emotional and psychological well-being. This is because modern life is filled with continuous social stimulation and stress, necessitating a quiet zone where a person can process their thoughts without interruption. For example, individuals who share small apartments often experience elevated anxiety if they do not have a private corner to unwind. Therefore, personal spaces act as vital psychological boundaries that maintain domestic harmony and support mental health.

04 Describe a place where you saw many animals

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where this place is; When you went there; What kind of animals you saw; And explain how you felt about this place.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'm going to share my experience of visiting Cuc Phuong National Park in Ninh Binh province, which is a highly biodiverse protected forest. I went there last spring during a school biology field trip. During our trek through the dense forest canopy, we encountered an incredible variety of animals in their natural habitat. We visited the Endangered Primate Rescue Center, where we saw rare langurs, gibbons, and loris. We also walked through the butterfly valley, which was filled with thousands of colorful butterflies fluttering around us. I felt extremely awestruck by this place because it was heart-warming to see the animals living in clean, spacious enclosures or roaming free in the forest, rather than being confined in small, dirty cages in captivity. It gave me a deep appreciation for wildlife conservation efforts and made me realize how crucial it is to protect these fragile ecosystems from human encroachment."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Biodiverse (adjective) /ˌbaɪəʊdaɪˈvɜːs/

Definition: Containing a wide variety of different species of plants and animals.
Collocations: Biodiverse ecosystem, highly biodiverse rainforest.
Example: National parks are biodiverse regions that must be protected from logging.

2. In captivity (prepositional phrase)

Definition: Kept in a confined space like a zoo or cage, rather than living free in the wild.
Collocations: Keep animals in captivity, bred in captivity.
Example: Some tigers born in captivity struggle to survive if they are released into the wild.

3. Conservation (noun) /ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃn/

Definition: The protection and preservation of natural resources, environments, and wildlife.
Collocations: Wildlife conservation, marine conservation.
Example: Crucial funding is needed to support global conservation programs for endangered species.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Should wild animals be kept in modern zoos?
Ans (OREO): From my perspective, modern zoos are acceptable only if they prioritize educational outreach, scientific research, and active wildlife rehabilitation. This is because standard entertainment-focused zoos often exploit animals for profit, whereas modern conservation parks strive to protect gene pools and reintroduce species back into the wild. For example, some specialized centers breed endangered primate species and release them into national parks once they are healthy. Therefore, zoos must evolve to become sanctuaries rather than mere tourist attractions.
