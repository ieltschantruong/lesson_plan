01 Describe a foreign country you would like to visit in the future (Page 60)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Which country it is; How you first learned about it; Who you would like to go there with; And explain why you would like to visit this country in the future.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A foreign country that has captured my imagination, and which I am determined to visit in the near future, is Japan. I first learned about Japan through travel documentaries on National Geographic when I was a teenager, and since then, my interest has grown exponentially. I would love to go there with my close friend who is an expert in photography. If we get the opportunity to travel there, I would love to explore the juxtaposition of its hyper-modern metropolises and its tranquil, revered historic sites. I want to experience the absolute hustle and bustle of Tokyo, but also escape to Kyoto's serene temples during the cherry blossom season. The primary reason for my desire to visit is the exquisite blend of cutting-edge technology and ancient traditions, which is beautifully preserved in Japanese culture. Additionally, I am an avid fan of culinary exploration, so embarking on a street-food tour to sample authentic sushi and ramen directly from local artisans is high on my bucket list. I believe this journey would offer a profound level of cultural immersion that would widen my horizons."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Juxtaposition (noun) /ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃn/

Definition: The fact of putting two things close together with contrasting effect.
Collocations: A striking juxtaposition, juxtaposition of old and new.
Example: The city's landscape presents an interesting juxtaposition of ancient temples and modern skyscrapers.

2. Metropolis (noun) /məˈtrɒpəlɪs/

Definition: A very large, busy, and important city, often the capital of a country or region.
Collocations: Bustling metropolis, sprawling metropolis.
Example: Tokyo is a massive metropolis that offers endless opportunities for exploration.

Part 3 Deep Dive (Page 60):

1. What do you think of national identity?

Ans (OREO): In my view, national identity is a vital shared sense of belonging and cultural values that defines a community. This is because having a clear collective heritage provides social cohesion and mutual support. For example, traditional celebrations bring different generations together, fostering unity. Therefore, maintaining national identity is crucial for cultural survival.

2. What are the advantages of working in an international company?

Ans (OREO): Working in a global firm offers exposure to diverse perspectives and advanced skills. This is because interacting with colleagues from different countries broadens one's professional horizons. For instance, employees learn international communication styles and strategies. Consequently, it accelerates career growth and global adaptability.

3. How does travelling abroad affect young people?

Ans (OREO): Travelling overseas significantly enhances young people's independence and maturity. This is due to the fact that navigating unfamiliar environments forces them to solve problems independently. For example, managing foreign currencies and maps builds confidence. Thus, international journeys build well-rounded global citizens.

4. How do people get information about other countries?

Ans (OREO): Today, the internet is the primary medium through which people explore the globe. This is because social media, blogs, and video channels provide real-time updates and visual insights. For instance, travel vlogs on YouTube offer raw, unedited glimpses of daily lives abroad. Hence, digital platforms have made global information highly accessible.

5. Do you think reading is a good way to get to know other countries? Why?

Ans (OREO): Yes, reading is an exceptional method to gain deep, reflective insights into foreign lands. Unlike videos, written material explores historical contexts and complex cultural nuances in detail. For example, reading local literature reveals the emotional and philosophical attitudes of a nation's people. Therefore, books provide a more profound understanding than superficial videos.

6. What do you think makes countries different?

Ans (OREO): In my view, unique historical developments and geographic climates are the key factors that differentiate countries. This is because geography dictates agriculture and housing, while history shapes language, customs, and laws. For instance, hot tropical regions develop open-air social habits, whereas colder climates favor cozy, indoor activities. Thus, a combination of history and environment crafts distinct nations.

02 Describe a city that you have been to and would like to visit again (Page 61)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What city it is; When you went there; What you did during your visit; What it was like; And explain why you would like to visit this city again.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A city that I have been to and absolutely yearn to visit again is Da Lat, a picturesque highland city in the southern parts of Vietnam. I went there about two years ago with my family to escape the sweltering heat of the summer season. During our stay, we did a variety of relaxing activities, such as renting a pedal boat on Xuan Huong Lake, wandering through the local night market, and visiting several colonial French villas hidden deep within the pine woods. It was an incredibly tranquil and misty place with ethereal morning fog. I would jump at the opportunity to visit Da Lat again because the slow pace of life and fresh mountain air acted as a highly therapeutic escape for me. It is the perfect place to decompress and recharge my batteries after a long semester of academic pressure."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Picturesque (adjective) /ˌpɪktʃəˈresk/

Definition: Visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way.
Example: Da Lat is famous for its picturesque valleys and pine forests.

2. Ethereal (adjective) /iˈθɪəriəl/

Definition: Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems not to be of this world.
Example: The early morning mist covering Xuan Huong Lake gave the city an ethereal beauty.

Part 3 Deep Dive (Page 61):

1. What are the main differences between urban and rural areas?

Ans (OREO): Urban areas are characterized by high-density populations, advanced infrastructure, and fast lifestyles, whereas rural settings offer natural landscapes and a slower pace. This is because cities center on commercial industries while villages rely on agriculture. For example, Tokyo is packed with subways and offices, whereas Hokkaido's countryside features vast farms. Thus, convenience and pace define their differences.

2. What is your opinion on the claim that large cities are well-suited for elderly residents?

Ans (OREO): I partially agree, as cities provide exceptional healthcare services, but they also introduce severe stress and pollution. This is due to the fact that top-tier hospitals are concentrated in urban areas, which is vital for seniors, yet noisy traffic can harm their well-being. For instance, an elderly person in Seoul has rapid medical access but struggles with air quality. Therefore, cities are suitable only if they incorporate peaceful green spaces.

3. Is it feasible for the entire population to migrate to urban areas?

Ans (OREO): No, complete urban migration is entirely unfeasible and dangerous. This is because cities would suffer from extreme overpopulation and resource depletion, while agricultural production would collapse. For example, if all farmers moved to cities, food shortages would immediately emerge. Hence, maintaining a balanced distribution of rural and urban populations is vital.

4. Do you believe rural residents tend to be more friendly compared to city dwellers?

Ans (OREO): Generally yes, rural communities foster closer social relationships and mutual trust. This is because smaller populations allow neighbors to know each other personally, whereas city life encourages individual isolation and privacy. For instance, village residents often greet strangers and share food, while city commuters ignore one another on subways. Therefore, rural environments naturally support communal friendliness.

5. Have you noticed any significant changes in your city recently?

Ans (OREO): Yes, my city has experienced rapid modernization and infrastructural expansion. This is because the local government is constructing new subway systems and green parks to accommodate urban growth. For example, high-speed rail links have recently connected peripheral areas to the commercial center. Consequently, commuting times have decreased, though traffic congestion remains an issue.

6. What measures should governments implement to enhance public safety?

Ans (OREO): Governments must invest in extensive surveillance systems and increase active police patrols. This is because visible security forces and clear street lighting actively discourage criminal activities. For instance, installing smart CCTV cameras in metropolitan alleys has dramatically reduced street crimes. Thus, high-tech monitoring and active policing are critical for security.

7. How do you think urbanization affects the environment and quality of life?

Ans (OREO): Urbanization significantly harms the local environment while offering mixed outcomes for quality of life. This is because industrial concentrations pollute air and water systems, yet cities provide excellent employment and education. For example, smog in megacities causes severe respiratory illnesses, despite advanced economic opportunities. Therefore, green urban planning is vital to balance these impacts.

8. What role do public transportation systems play in modern city development?

Ans (OREO): Public transit systems are the literal lifeblood of sustainable modern cities. This is because reliable trains and buses minimize carbon emissions and reduce individual car dependencies. For instance, a well-connected metro network allows thousands of commuters to travel daily without clogging expressways. Consequently, transit networks are essential for clean, productive cities.

9. How can cities better accommodate the needs of growing populations?

Ans (OREO): Cities must develop vertical residential structures and implement smart utility systems. This is because physical space is restricted, necessitating efficient, high-density housing layouts. For example, high-rise apartments integrated with rainwater harvesting reduce geographic footprints and water stress. Thus, vertical and ecological housing models are key solutions.

10. What impact does city planning have on community relationships?

Ans (OREO): Well-structured city planning actively fosters strong communal ties and social trust. This is due to the fact that integrating public squares, parks, and pedestrian zones encourages face-to-face interactions. For instance, neighborhoods with central community gardens show higher levels of mutual cooperation and safety. Therefore, inclusive layouts build cohesive societies.

11. Should governments focus more on developing rural areas or improving existing cities?

Ans (OREO): Governments should prioritize rural development to decentralize resources and control overpopulation. This is because modernizing village schools and hospitals discourages young residents from flocking to crowded metropolises. For example, establishing digital workspaces in agricultural zones allows remote jobs to flourish locally. Thus, rural enrichment creates sustainable countrywide equilibrium.

03 Describe your favorite place in your home where you like to relax (Page 62)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where this place is; What it looks like; What you enjoy doing there; And explain why you feel relaxed there.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"My absolute favorite place in my home where I go to wind down is the small balcony connected to my bedroom. It's a cozy, secluded space that looks out onto our quiet backyard garden. Over the last couple of years, I’ve decorated it with several potted herbs, hanging ivy, and a soft, comfortable wooden armchair. Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I sit out there with a hot cup of tea and a good novel. Sometimes, I simply put on my headphones, play some ambient music, and listen to the birds. This balcony acts as a highly personal sanctuary where I can decompress and shut out the noise of the outside world. I love it because it’s quiet, private, and filled with natural sunlight. It offers an invaluable environment for quiet contemplation, making it the perfect spot to find my peace of mind."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Sanctuary (noun) /ˈsæŋktʃuəri/

Definition: A place of safety, refuge, or holiness, where one can find peace from danger or difficulty.
Example: After a hectic day at work, my library room becomes my private sanctuary.

2. Decompress (verb) /ˌdiːkəmˈpres/

Definition: To relax and release tension or pressure after a period of hard work or stress.
Example: Taking a hot shower and listening to classical music helps me decompress quickly.

Part 3 Deep Dive (Page 62):

1. Why is it difficult for some people to relax?

Ans (OREO): Modern professionals struggle to unwind due to constant work notifications and digital noise. This is because smartphones blur the boundaries between office hours and personal life, keeping the mind alert. For example, answering emails at midnight prevents deep cognitive rest. Thus, digital connectivity hinders relaxation.

2. What are the benefits of relaxation?

Ans (OREO): Mindful relaxation significantly reduces stress hormones and restores emotional resilience. This is because resting lowers heart rates and lets neural pathways repair. For instance, meditation has been shown to boost creative problem-solving and memory focus. Therefore, resting is vital for long-term health and productivity.

3. Do people have enough time for relaxation nowadays?

Ans (OREO): No, contemporary lifestyles are heavily packed with commercial demands and domestic tasks. This is due to intense corporate competition and long daily commutes that deplete free hours. For example, working parents often spend their weekends doing household chores instead of resting. Hence, modern schedules leave little room for actual leisure.

4. How do people usually relax?

Ans (OREO): Relaxation methods vary widely, ranging from consuming passive digital entertainment to practicing quiet outdoor hobbies. This is because some seek slow distraction while others favor physical or sensory changes. For instance, many teenagers scroll social feeds to relax, whereas older adults prefer gardening. Thus, leisure preferences are highly subjective.

5. Is physical activity good for relaxation?

Ans (OREO): Absolutely, physical exercises are highly efficient for easing mental strain. This is because strenuous movement stimulates the endocrine system to release endorphins, which act as natural mood elevators. For example, jogging after a stressful day quickly dispels cognitive fatigue. Therefore, bodily action directly promotes mental ease.

6. Do you think that spending time alone is important for relaxation?

Ans (OREO): Yes, solitude is indispensable for actual cognitive recovery and self-reflection. This is because social interactions, even with close friends, require continuous mental focus and compromise. For instance, sitting silently in a room lets a person process raw feelings without social pressures. Thus, alone-time is critical to restore energy.

7. What are the benefits of doing exercise?

Ans (OREO): Consistent physical exercise enhances cardiovascular health, strengthens muscles, and boosts cognitive longevity. This is because structured workouts improve blood circulation, which supplies more oxygen to brain cells. For example, active seniors show far lower risks of memory disorders. Therefore, movement guarantees overall physical and mental durability.

8. Do people in your country exercise after work?

Ans (OREO): Yes, a growing number of health-conscious urbanites actively exercise in local parks or gyms after office hours. This is due to rising awareness about sedentary lifestyles and obesity risks. For example, in Hanoi, public parks are packed with joggers and aerobics groups every evening. Thus, after-work workouts have become a solid health trend.

9. What is the place where people spend most of their time at home?

Ans (OREO): Generally, the living room is the primary hub where family members gather and spend hours. This is because it serves as the central space for shared activities like watching television, dining, and talking. For instance, families typically sit together on the living room sofa after dinner. Thus, the lounge area is the home's social center.

10. Do you think there should be classes for training young people and children how to relax?

Ans (OREO): Yes, integrating mindfulness and breathing exercises into school schedules is highly beneficial today. This is because modern children face unprecedented academic pressures and screen-related anxieties. For example, short meditation classes help children manage exam stress and control temper outbreaks. Therefore, emotional relaxation training is critical for youth.

11. Which is more important, mental relaxation or physical relaxation?

Ans (OREO): Both are inextricably linked, but I believe mental relaxation is slightly more critical, as cognitive stress directly triggers physical illness. When the mind is constantly anxious, it leads to chronic physical fatigue, muscle pain, and poor sleep. For instance, meditation can alleviate actual bodily tension, whereas sleeping with an anxious mind offers no real rest. Thus, mental tranquility is the core foundation.

12. How has technology affected the way people relax and unwind?

Ans (OREO): Technology has made entertainment highly accessible, but it has also compromised the quality of our relaxation. This is because scrolling social media keeps our brains active with blue light and information overload, rather than letting it rest. For example, watching videos in bed often delays natural sleep cycles. Therefore, digital leisure is often a counterproductive distraction.

13. What role does music play in helping people relax?

Ans (OREO): Music serves as an exceptionally powerful therapeutic tool to adjust our emotional states. Slow, harmonic rhythms directly slow down heart rates and reduce anxiety levels. For example, playing classical or ambient sounds during commutes helps busy professionals unwind instantly. Thus, auditory harmonies are deeply beneficial for our nerves.

14. Do you think modern lifestyles make it harder for people to find time to relax?

Ans (OREO): Yes, modern lifestyles are dominated by extreme economic competition and a constant urge to remain productive. This "hustle culture" associates quiet relaxation with guilt and lazy behavior. For instance, people often feel pressured to study or side-hustle during their free weekends. Consequently, our minds are rarely allowed to rest completely.

15. What are some traditional methods of relaxation in your culture?

Ans (OREO): In Vietnamese culture, traditional relaxation is heavily centered on slow, social interactions and tea appreciation. This involves gathering at rustic lakeside cafes to slowly sip local green tea or herbal infusions with friends. For example, older generations spend hours playing Chinese chess in parks under tree shade. Therefore, traditional relaxation focuses on simplicity and connection.

16. How can companies help their employees maintain a healthy work-life balance?

Ans (OREO): Firms must enforce strict policies regarding after-hours communications and offer flexible working structures. This is because banning professional emails on weekends lets employees disconnect mentally from their duties. For example, some companies block server access during holidays to guarantee family time. Thus, institutional boundaries are necessary for balance.

04 Describe a place where you saw many animals (Page 63)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: When you went there; Who you went with; What animals you saw there; And explain how you felt about this place.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'm going to share my experience of visiting Cuc Phuong National Park in Ninh Binh province, which is a highly biodiverse protected forest. I went there last spring during a school biology field trip with my classmates. During our trek through the dense forest canopy, we encountered an incredible variety of animals in their natural habitat. We visited the Endangered Primate Rescue Center, where we saw rare langurs, gibbons, and loris. We also walked through the butterfly valley, which was filled with thousands of colorful butterflies fluttering around us. I felt extremely awestruck by this place because it was heart-warming to see the animals living in clean, spacious enclosures or roaming free in the forest, rather than being confined in small, dirty cages in captivity. It gave me a deep appreciation for wildlife conservation efforts and made me realize how crucial it is to protect these fragile ecosystems from human encroachment."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Biodiverse (adjective) /ˌbaɪəʊdaɪˈvɜːs/

Definition: Containing a wide variety of different species of plants and animals.
Example: National parks are biodiverse regions that must be protected from logging.

2. In captivity (prepositional phrase)

Definition: Kept in a confined space like a zoo or cage, rather than living free in the wild.
Example: Some tigers born in captivity struggle to survive if they are released into the wild.

Part 3 Deep Dive (Page 63):

1. Why do stories and movies for children always feature animals?

Ans (OREO): In my view, animals serve as highly neutral and relatable characters that transcend cultural and social barriers for kids. This is because young children are naturally drawn to nature, and animal figures simplify moral messages without complex human prejudices. For instance, animated films like "The Lion King" teach loyalty and responsibility through animal groups. Thus, animal characters make stories universally accessible.

2. How did animals help people in the past?

Ans (OREO): In historical times, animals represented the primary source of labor, transportation, and agricultural production. This was due to the absence of modern mechanized machinery, making humans entirely reliant on domestic animals. For example, buffaloes were indispensable for plowing muddy rice fields in Asian societies. Therefore, animals were vital for basic human survival.

3. Do you think people are more interested in animals now than they were in the past?

Ans (OREO): Yes, modern interest in animals has shifted from basic utility to emotional connection and environmental conservation. While ancestors viewed animals strictly as labor or food sources, modern societies treat them as emotional companions and focus on preserving species. For example, millions of families keep pets as actual family members. Hence, our interest is more empathetic today.

4. How do animals help us today?

Ans (OREO): Today, animals provide essential emotional companionship, support scientific research, and assist people with medical disabilities. This is because their presence reduces psychological stress and their keen senses can be trained for medical assistance. For instance, guide dogs allow visually impaired individuals to navigate cities safely. Thus, they support our psychological and physical needs.

5. Why should we protect animals?

Ans (OREO): Safeguarding animals is vital to maintain global ecological balances and ensure our own long-term survival. This is due to the fact that every species plays a specific role in food chains, and losing one can collapse entire ecosystems. For example, a sudden drop in bee populations immediately threatens global crop pollination. Therefore, protecting wildlife is an ecological necessity.

6. Do you think children love animals more than old people do?

Ans (OREO): Not necessarily; they express their affection in entirely different ways. Children love animals out of pure curiosity and playful excitement, whereas elderly people seek steady, silent companionship to ease loneliness. For example, a child enjoys running around with a dog, while a senior finds comfort in simply petting a cat. Thus, both age brackets value animals deeply.

7. What are the benefits of keeping pets for families?

Ans (OREO): Keeping domestic pets teaches children responsibility while acting as a natural stress reliever for parents. This is because caring for a pet requires regular schedules (like feeding and walking) which builds discipline. For instance, children who manage pets show higher levels of empathy in social setups. Therefore, pets strengthen domestic harmony and emotional growth.

8. How do zoos contribute to animal conservation?

Ans (OREO): Modern zoos protect endangered species through structured breeding programs and raise public conservation awareness. This is because standard zoos help prevent extinction of critically vulnerable animals and fund research. For example, breeding programs for giant pandas have successfully increased their global numbers. Hence, modern zoos serve as vital scientific centers.

9. Should wild animals be used for entertainment purposes?

Ans (OREO): No, utilizing wild animals in circuses or commercial shows is highly unethical and cruel. This is because training them often involves physical abuse, and keeping them in restricted spaces causes severe psychological trauma. For instance, dolphins in amusement parks display high stress due to chlorination and confined pools. Thus, wild entertainment should be universally banned.

10. What can people do to help endangered species?

Ans (OREO): Individuals can help by avoiding wildlife products, reducing carbon footprints, and funding reliable conservation charities. This is because stopping demand for ivory or exotic furs directly prevents illegal poaching. For example, refusing to buy coral ornaments protects delicate marine ecosystems. Therefore, conscious consumer choices are highly effective tools.
