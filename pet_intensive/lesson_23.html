01 Describe an interesting traditional story

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What the story is; How you first learned about it; Who the main characters are; And explain why you find this traditional story so interesting.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd like to talk about a highly fascinating traditional Vietnamese legend known as 'The Legend of Saint Giong' or 'Thanh Giong.' I first learned about this story in early childhood, when my grandmother would narrate it as a bedtime tale. The main character is a three-year-old boy who, despite his age, had never spoken or walked. However, when the nation faced a massive foreign invasion, he suddenly stood up, grew into a giant, rode a flaming iron horse, and brandished a sword to single-handedly defeat the invaders. I think this story is deeply interesting because Giong did not fight for personal glory or a royal title; after securing victory, he immediately flew up to the heavens, leaving behind a legacy of absolute selflessness. It beautifully captures the spirit of national unity and heroic patriotism that has defined our cultural history for centuries."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Narrate (verb) /ˈnær.eɪt/

Definition: To tell a story or describe a series of events.
Collocations: Narrate a tale, narrate in detail.
Example: Elders used to narrate folk tales to pass historical values to grandchildren.

2. Brandish (verb) /ˈbræn.dɪʃ/

Definition: To wave something (especially a weapon) in a triumphant, aggressive, or excited way.
Example: In the traditional play, the actor brandished a golden sword to symbolize victory.

3. Blessing in disguise (idiom)

Definition: A misfortune or obstacle that eventually results in a highly positive outcome.
Example: The bamboo forest being burned down was a blessing in disguise as it provided Saint Giong with perfect makeshift weapons.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why is it important to preserve folk tales and traditional legends in a modern, digital society?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, safeguarding cultural myths is paramount for preserving national identity. This is because folk tales encapsulate a society's core values, historical struggles, and moral compass that digital platforms cannot replicate. For example, stories like Saint Giong teach children courage and collective responsibility rather than individualism. Therefore, preserving these narratives prevents cultural erosion and binds communities together across generations.

02 Describe a kind of foreign food you like and you have had

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What the food is; Where you had it; How often you eat it; And explain why you enjoy this foreign food so much.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I would love to share my passion for authentic Japanese Ramen, particularly Tonkotsu Ramen, which I first tasted at a small, cozy noodle bar in Ho Chi Minh City about a year ago. It has since become a monthly culinary treat for me. This dish consists of wheat noodles served in a rich, velvety pork bone broth that has been simmered for hours, topped with tender chashu pork, a soft-boiled egg, and seaweed. What makes this foreign food so enjoyable is the striking complexity of its flavors. The broth has an intensely savory, umami profile that completely blankets the palate. Eating a steaming bowl of ramen is not just a basic meal; it is a highly therapeutic and comforting experience, especially during the rainy season. It represents a beautiful culinary masterpiece where every single ingredient has been prepared with extreme precision."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Velvety (adjective) /ˈvel.və.ti/

Definition: Having a smooth, soft, and luxurious texture or flavor.
Collocations: Velvety texture, velvety broth.
Example: The slow-simmered bone broth had a velvety consistency that warmed me instantly.

2. Umami (noun/adjective) /uːˈmɑː.mi/

Definition: A category of taste in food corresponding to the flavor of glutamates, characterized as savory or meaty.
Example: Mushrooms, seaweed, and fermented soy are highly praised for their natural umami.

3. Suit one's palate (idiom)

Definition: To be highly agreeable, delicious, or satisfying to someone's personal taste.
Example: While some find raw seafood strange, Japanese sushi perfectly suits my palate.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How has the globalization of cuisine affected local culinary traditions in urban areas?
Ans (OREO): From my perspective, food globalization has acted as a double-edged sword. This is because while it introduces urbanites to incredibly diverse palates, it occasionally threatens the survival of traditional, low-margin street stalls. For instance, the rise of international fast-food chains has led younger generations to neglect local historical dishes. Therefore, while culinary diversity should be embraced, local governments must actively support and promote traditional gastronomy to maintain cultural heritage.

03 Describe a person from a different cultural background with whom you enjoy spending time

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Who this person is; How you met; What you do together; And explain why you enjoy spending time with him/her.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd love to describe my good friend, Sarah, who is an exchange student from Germany. We crossed paths last autumn during our university's global orientation week, and we hit it off immediately. Despite our diverse upbringings, we share a deep passion for history and hiking. On weekends, we often explore old temples around Hue or hike up nearby hills, trading stories about our respective nations. I thoroughly enjoy spending time with Sarah because she acts as an intellectual mirror. Her direct, analytical European perspective always challenges my assumptions and offers a striking contrast to our more indirect communication styles. Speaking with her is an absolute masterclass in cross-cultural empathy. She is extremely open-minded, and our endless discussions over cups of local salt coffee have definitely broadened my horizons and dissolved several of my preconceived notions about Western lifestyles."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Cross-cultural (adjective) /ˌkrɒsˈkʌl.tʃər.əl/

Definition: Relating to a comparison or interaction between different cultures.
Example: Participating in exchange programs fosters essential cross-cultural communication skills.

2. Broaden one's horizons (idiom)

Definition: To expand someone's range of knowledge, interests, or general experience.
Example: Traveling solo and interacting with locals will significantly broaden your horizons.

3. Preconceived notion (noun phrase)

Definition: An opinion or belief formed beforehand without actual experience or evidence.
Example: We should approach new communities without any preconceived notions.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why are cross-cultural friendships highly beneficial for university students?
Ans (OREO): In my view, fostering friendships across diverse backgrounds is essential for preparing students for a globalized workforce. This is because daily interactions break down systemic stereotypes and nurture high-level interpersonal adaptability. For instance, collaborating with international peers on group projects teaches students how to navigate varying communication styles and expectations. Therefore, cross-cultural friendships develop robust global citizens who possess high cultural intelligence.

04 Describe a place with a lot of trees that you would like to visit (e.g. a forest)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where this place is; What it looks like; Who you would like to go with; And explain why you would like to visit this place with trees.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"A majestic wooded area that I am incredibly eager to visit is the ancient Yakushima Forest, located on a remote island in southern Japan. This magical place is world-renowned for its primeval, moss-draped cedar trees, some of which are thousands of years old. The forest floor is described as an otherworldly carpet of vibrant green ferns and moss-covered roots, with thick mist frequently weaving through the massive canopy. I would love to explore this natural sanctuary alongside my younger sister, who is an aspiring landscape photographer. The primary reason I yearn to visit Yakushima is to experience 'Shinrin-yoku' or forest bathing. In our fast-paced, screen-dominated urban lives, I find myself completely drained. I believe walking in such a serene, historic wilderness would have a highly therapeutic, grounding effect, helping me decompress and reconnect with the quiet majesty of our planet's pristine ecosystems."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Primeval (adjective) /praɪˈmiː.vəl/

Definition: Belonging to the earliest times in the history of the world; ancient and undisturbed by human activity.
Example: Walking through the primeval jungle felt like traveling back millions of years.

2. Canopy (noun) /ˈkæn.ə.pi/

Definition: The high, leafy roof formed by the uppermost branches of trees in a forest.
Collocations: Dense canopy, forest canopy.
Example: The thick jungle canopy blocked out almost all sunlight, keeping the forest floor cool.

3. Forest bathing (noun phrase)

Definition: The practice of spending calm, quiet time in a forest to improve physical and mental health (derived from Japanese *Shinrin-yoku*).
Example: Doctors frequently recommend forest bathing as a natural way to reduce stress levels.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why are municipal governments investing in creating small 'pocket forests' in dense urban spaces?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, introducing mini-forests into concrete jungles is highly beneficial. This is because urban trees act as natural air-purifying filters, mitigating the heat island effect while offering citizens an essential psychological retreat. For example, pocket forests in Tokyo have successfully lowered local ambient temperatures while reducing resident stress levels. Therefore, urban forestry is a vital component of sustainable, liveable city planning.
