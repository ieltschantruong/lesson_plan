Task 1 Table Analysis

"The graph below shows the percentage of adults in urban and rural areas who took part in four types of activities in 1990 and 2000. Summarize the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant."
Brainstormed Ideas & Structure
1. Introduction

Paraphrase the prompt: The table contrasts the percentages of urban and rural adults participating in four different leisure activities (reading, animated play, playing, and photography) across two distinct years: 1990 and 2000.

2. Overview

Highlight key trends: Reading was by far the most popular activity for both urban and rural demographics across both years, ending in an equal peak of 78% in 2000. Photography saw notable increases in both areas, while animated play remained the least common pastime.

3. Body Paragraph 1 (Reading & Photography)
  • Reading: High interest overall. Urban rates rose from 61% in 1990 to 78% in 2000. Rural rates also climbed from 71% to match the urban peak at 78% by 2000.
  • Photography: Showed prominent growth. Urban rates grew from 7% to 17%, while rural participation nearly doubled, surging from 14% to 26%.
4. Body Paragraph 2 (Animated Play & Playing)
  • Animated Play: Remained completely static at 10% for urban areas. Rural participation decreased slightly from 18% in 1990 to 14% in 2000.
  • Playing: Exhibited a downward trend in urban sectors, falling from 21% to 14%. However, rural rates remained entirely static at 26% across both years.
Sample Band 8.0 Response

The table compares the percentages of adults residing in urban and rural settings who engaged in four types of leisure activities—reading, animated play, playing, and photography—during the years 1990 and 2000.

Overall, reading was consistently the most popular activity for both urban and rural adults across both years, experiencing noticeable growth to reach an equal peak by 2000. Additionally, photography saw upward trends in both demographics, while animated play remained the least preferred pastime. Playing witnessed stable participation in rural areas but dropped in urban centers.

In terms of reading and photography, reading was highly favored, starting at 61% for urban dwellers and 71% for rural residents in 1990, before climbing to a tied peak of 78% in 2000. Photography also experienced significant growth over the ten-year period, with urban participation rising from 7% to 17%, and rural engagement nearly doubling from 14% to 26%.

Concerning animated play and playing, animated play remained completely unchanged at 10% in urban areas, while rural participation decreased slightly from 18% in 1990 to 14% in 2000. Meanwhile, engagement in playing exhibited divergent trends; urban participation declined significantly from 21% to 14%, whereas rural playing rates remained completely static at 26% across both years.

Task 2 Essay

"Some professionals, such as teachers and doctors make a great contribution to society so they should be paid more than sports athletes and entertainment stars. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
Brainstormed Ideas & Structure
1. Introduction

Paraphrase hook: The income disparity between public service professionals (doctors, educators) and entertainment celebrities (athletes, actors). State thesis: While public servants deserve vastly superior baseline remuneration due to their foundational contributions, a direct comparison is complex due to market-driven commercial revenues.

2. Body Paragraph 1 (Why Public Servants Deserve Higher Pay)
  • Societal Foundation: Doctors save lives, and teachers cultivate human capital. Society cannot function sustainably without them.
  • Economic Stability: Underpaid teachers and doctors lead to brain drain, deteriorating healthcare systems, and educational decay.
3. Body Paragraph 2 (Why Celebrities Earn More - Market Reality)
  • Commercial Scaling: A surgeon can save one patient at a time, but a movie star or soccer player entertains millions simultaneously, generating massive advertisement, broadcasting, and merchandise revenues.
  • Short-lived Careers: Athletic and media careers are highly volatile, brief, and prone to career-ending injuries.
4. Conclusion

Summarize: While commercial market forces explain why celebrities command astronomical wages, governments must subsidize and heavily elevate the compensation of healthcare and educational professionals to reflect their intrinsic, monumental value.

Sample Band 8.0 Response

The vast earnings gap between essential public professionals, such as educators and physicians, and elite entertainment figures, including sports stars and actors, has sparked intense debate. While I agree that the baseline salaries of teachers and doctors should be heavily increased to match their monumental societal contributions, I believe that the astronomical income of stars is a result of market-driven dynamics that cannot be easily regulated by standard wage caps.

On the one hand, public sector professionals form the bedrock of any stable civilization. Doctors are responsible for safeguarding public health and preserving human lives, while teachers nurture the intellectual capital of future generations. Without their expertise, a country would face severe educational decline and systemic health crises. Underpaying these crucial pillars leads to a shortage of talent and brain drain, as talented graduates avoid these fields in favor of more lucrative corporate careers. Therefore, the moral and practical value of these services dictates that they should receive superior compensation packages from public-sector budgets.

On the other hand, the multi-million dollar paychecks of athletes and entertainers are dictated by the economics of scale and consumer demand. Unlike a doctor, who can only treat one patient at a time, a soccer star or a prominent musician can entertain millions of viewers simultaneously through digital media, broadcasting, and merchandise. This immense reach generates colossal revenues from advertising, tickets, and television rights. Because celebrities represent the primary revenue-driving asset for their respective commercial industries, their high income is a proportional share of the profits they generate. Additionally, the career span of a professional athlete or entertainer is typically brief and volatile, requiring them to accumulate wealth in a highly restricted period.

In conclusion, while market-driven forces explain why entertainment stars command such vast fortunes, this does not excuse the systemic underfunding of essential public services. Governments must aggressively subsidize and elevate the remuneration of healthcare and education professionals to reflect their true value, even if they can never realistically match the commercially generated wealth of global celebrities.
