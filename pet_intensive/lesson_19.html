64 Describe a shop/store you enjoy visiting

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What shop it is; Where it is; What they sell; And explain why you enjoy visiting this shop.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"An retail store that I absolutely adore visiting is a quaint, independent vintage bookstore nestled in a quiet, leafy alley of Hue's city center. It's called 'The Cozy Nook'. As its name suggests, the shop is a haven of peace, selling rare, second-hand English literature, historical archives, and beautiful local hand-crafted stationary. The interior layout is wonderfully aesthetic, with old oak bookcases extending from floor to ceiling, and the scent of aged paper filling the air. I thoroughly enjoy visiting this store primarily because of its exceptionally atmospheric environment, which acts as a sanctuary from the hectic pace of daily life. The owner is a true bibliophile who always curates a remarkable selection and provides a comfortable reading corner with free hot jasmine tea. I often find myself completely losing track of time there, browsing through timeless classics."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Bibliophile (noun) /ˈbɪbliəʊfaɪl/

Definition: A person who collects or has a great love of books.
Collocations: Avid bibliophile, true bibliophile.
Example: The bookstore owner is a true bibliophile who knows the background of every single book on his shelf.

2. Atmospheric (adjective) /ˌætməsˈferɪk/

Definition: Creating an exciting, pleasant, or unique mood or feeling in a place.
Example: The warm lightning and soft background jazz make the antique store exceptionally atmospheric.

3. Lose track of time (idiom)

Definition: To be so engrossed in an activity that you do not notice the passage of time.
Example: Whenever I enter that vintage store, I completely lose track of time and miss my appointments.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Do you think e-commerce will completely replace brick-and-mortar stores in the future?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, although online shopping offers unmatched convenience, physical retail outlets will never be entirely replaced. This is because brick-and-mortar establishments provide tactile sensory experiences and social interactions that digital screens cannot replicate. For instance, consumers prefer to feel the fabrics of clothing or try on products physically before committing to a purchase. Therefore, while virtual stores will dominate bulk purchasing, physical shops will survive by transforming into experiential hubs.

65 Describe an article on health you read in a magazine or on the Internet

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Where you read it; What it was about; Why you read it; And explain what you learned from this article.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"Recently, while scrolling through my daily digital feeds, I stumbled upon a highly fascinating article on sleep hygiene on a prominent medical website called Healthline. The article was titled 'The Science of Circadian Rhythm and Cognitive Recovery.' I read it out of pure necessity; I had been struggling with insomnia and daytime fatigue for months due to academic pressure. The article detailed how our internal biological clock is affected by the blue light emitted from our smartphones. It offered invaluable insights into how sleeping in complete darkness can optimize melatonin production. What I learned was absolutely mind-blowing. I discovered that consistent sleeping schedules are far more crucial for memory consolidation than the actual duration of sleep. Implementing the suggestions from the article, such as banning devices an hour before bed, has made a world of difference to my overall energy and productivity."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Sleep hygiene (noun phrase)

Definition: Habits and practices that are conducive to sleeping well on a regular basis.
Example: Avoiding caffeine late in the afternoon is a key rule of good sleep hygiene.

2. Melatonin (noun) /ˌmeləˈtəʊnɪn/

Definition: A hormone secreted by the pineal gland which inhibits melanin formation and is thought to be concerned with regulating the reproductive cycle.
Collocations: Optimize melatonin production, melatonin secretion.
Example: Blue screen light blocks the body's natural release of melatonin, making it difficult to wind down.

3. Invaluable insights (noun phrase)

Definition: Extremely useful and precious understandings or observations.
Example: The health article provided invaluable insights into the link between diet and mental health.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why has health information on social media become highly popular but also potentially dangerous?
Ans (OREO): From my perspective, health content on social media is popular because it is visual and digestible, but it is risky due to a complete lack of peer review. This is because self-proclaimed wellness gurus often promote extreme diets and untested supplements to gain followers and monetize their channels. For instance, the recent viral trend of dry fasting has led to severe cases of dehydration. Therefore, while accessible health information is beneficial, users must verify all digital claims with qualified medical professionals.

66 Describe a time when you saw something in the sky (e.g. flying kites, birds, sunset, etc.)

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: When and where you saw it; Who you were with; What you saw; And explain how you felt about it.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I want to share my experience of witnessing a spectacular, otherworldly sunset while sitting with my younger sister by West Lake in Hanoi. This happened on a warm, clear Sunday evening last autumn. As we sat on the lakeside benches, the sky gradually transformed into a vibrant kaleidoscope of colors. The clouds were illuminated with rich shades of deep orange, pastel pink, and royal violet. What made this sky view particularly mesmerizing was the reflection of the celestial light on the vast, calm surface of the lake, which created a perfect mirror effect. I felt an immediate sense of tranquil peace and quiet contemplation. In our bustling, hyper-connected cities, we rarely pause to appreciate the simple majesty of nature. It was a hypnotic, grounding moment that made me realize how important it is to disconnect and enjoy the fleeting, natural beauty of our world."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Kaleidoscope of colors (noun phrase)

Definition: A multi-colored pattern or display that changes continuously.
Example: The autumn canopy presented a breathtaking kaleidoscope of colors from bright red to deep amber.

2. Celestial (adjective) /səˈlestʃl/

Definition: Positioned in or relating to the sky, or outer space as observed in astronomy; heavenly.
Example: Star gazing in the mountains offered an unobstructed view of various celestial bodies.

3. Hypnotic (adjective) /hɪpˈnɒtɪk/

Definition: Producing a state of hypnosis; fascinating or captivating in a repetitive, soothing way.
Example: Watching the rhythmic movement of the sea waves has a highly hypnotic and therapeutic effect.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why do many modern metropolitan residents suffer from a lack of astronomical sky views?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, this is primarily caused by light pollution and dense smog in major urban areas. This is because high-density commercial centers emit continuous artificial glow from skyscrapers and billboards, which completely overpowers the delicate celestial light of stars. For example, in cities like Ho Chi Minh City, it is nearly impossible to see any constellations at night. Therefore, urban planning must incorporate dark-sky preserves in ecological parks to reconnect city dwellers with astronomical wonders.

67 Describe a gift you would like to give to someone

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What the gift is; Who you would like to give it to; Why you chose this gift; And explain how you think this person would react.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I would love to describe a highly thoughtful gift that I plan to give to my best friend, Huy, on his upcoming departure for graduate studies in the UK. The gift is a hand-crafted, vintage-style leather-bound travel journal. Inside, the pages are made of thick, textured ivory paper, and I plan to engrave his initials on the cover alongside a personalized quote. I chose this specific gift because Huy has an insatiable wanderlust and loves documenting his journeys with sketches and handwritten notes. This journal acts as an elegant, analog sanctuary for his thoughts in an increasingly digitized world. I am absolutely certain that the gift will be warmly received. Knowing Huy's sentimental nature, he will probably be deeply touched and value it as a precious keepsake. It will serve as a constant, tangible reminder of our long-term friendship while he navigates his life abroad."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Insatiable wanderlust (noun phrase)

Definition: An extremely strong and constant desire to travel and explore the world.
Example: Her insatiable wanderlust has led her to backpack across four different continents in two years.

2. Keepsake (noun) /ˈkiːpseɪk/

Definition: A small item kept in memory of the person who gave it or originally owned it.
Collocations: Sentimental keepsake, treasured keepsake.
Example: She gave me her grandmother's silver ring as a keepsake before she left for Australia.

3. Warmly received (verb phrase)

Definition: Welcomed or accepted with immense joy, enthusiasm, or kindness.
Example: The newly published historical novel was warmly received by both literary critics and avid readers.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why has commercialization shifted focus from the emotional value of gifts to their actual price tag?
Ans (OREO): In my view, the rise of mass-consumerism has conditioned societies to equate material cost with affection. This is because modern advertising aggressively suggests that expensive luxury goods reflect a higher tier of care or status. For instance, children often feel let down if they receive hand-crafted toys instead of the latest expensive digital console. Therefore, we must actively cultivate gift-giving values centered on thought, effort, and emotional relevance rather than superficial luxury.

68 Describe your first day at school that you remember

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: How old you were; What school it was; What happened on that day; And explain how you felt about your first day.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I have an incredibly vivid memory of my first day at elementary school. I was exactly six years old, and the school was a traditional, yellow-walled public academy in Hue. On that morning, my mother dressed me in my crisp white uniform and walked with me through the bustling school gates. The experience was initially nerve-wracking; the sheer volume of energetic kids running around and shouting was completely overwhelming. I clung to my mother's hand like a lifesaver. However, my anxiety faded when my homeroom teacher welcomed me with an atmosphere of warmth and kindness. She led me to my desk, introduced me to my seatmate, and initiated some highly fun group-bonding games. By the afternoon, I was laughing and playing with my new classmates. In retrospect, that day is impossible to erase from my memory because it marked a crucial milestone, transitioning from my family cocoon into a structured, educational landscape."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Vivid memory (noun phrase)

Definition: A clear, detailed, and highly realistic mental recollection of a past event.
Example: I still have a vivid memory of my first flight to Japan when I was ten.

2. Nerve-wracking (adjective) /ˈnɜːv rækɪŋ/

Definition: Causing extreme stress, anxiety, or mental tension.
Collocations: Nerve-wracking experience, nerve-wracking interview.
Example: Speaking in front of a massive audience of five hundred professionals was absolutely nerve-wracking.

3. Erase from memory (idiom)

Definition: To completely forget or remove a past recollection from one's mind.
Example: The tragic accident was a horrific event that he wished he could erase from his memory.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How do early childhood experiences at elementary school shape an individual's lifelong perception of academic study?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, the initial experiences children have in formal education lay the cornerstone for their future learning attitudes. This is because a supportive, encouraging, and engaging environment fosters a growth mindset, associating academic tasks with pleasure and curiosity. For example, children who are constantly praised for effort by kind teachers often develop high academic resilience in high school. Therefore, educational institutions must invest heavily in training kindergarten and primary teachers to facilitate highly positive first impressions.
