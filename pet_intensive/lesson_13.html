Speaking Part 2: Individual Long Turn (1-2 Mins)

What is Part 2?

The examiner gives you a task card with a specific topic and 3-4 bullet points. You have **1 minute to prepare** and take notes, and you must **speak continuously for 1 to 2 minutes**.

The PPF Strategy (Past-Present-Future)

If you struggle to speak for the full 2 minutes, structure your content across time horizons to expand your response effortlessly:

  • Past Context: How did you first discover this topic/experience? (e.g., "Initially, back when I was a student...")
  • Present Reality: What is the current situation? Describe the details, people, and objects involved using sensory adjectives.
  • Future Outlook: What are your plans, hopes, or predictions regarding this topic in the future?
Note-Taking Strategy (The 1-Minute Rule)

Do not write full sentences. Write **keywords, powerful adjectives, and 2-3 advanced idioms**. Organize notes in a quick spider-diagram representing the core bullet points.

Speaking Part 3: Deep Analytical Discussion (4-5 Mins)

What is Part 3?

A two-way conversational discussion with the examiner exploring abstract, conceptual topics linked directly to the theme of Part 2. Focus shifts from *personal* experiences to *general/societal* perspectives.

The OREO Expansion Framework

Structure every response into a coherent logical chain to demonstrate high coherence and cohesion (CC):

  • Opinion (O): Express a clear, immediate stance. (e.g., "From my perspective, it is highly likely that...")
  • Reason (R): Explain *why* you hold this stance. Use cause-and-effect connectors (e.g., "This is primarily because...", "As a consequence...")
  • Example (E): Provide a concrete illustration, statistic, or observation from society. (e.g., "To illustrate, in many metropolitan areas...")
  • Opinion Restated/Link (O): Conclude and tie the thought together. (e.g., "Therefore, it is evident that...")
Key Lexical Markers

For Delaying / Thinking:

"That is an intriguing question, let me ponder that for a moment..."

For Expressing Contrast:

"On the flip side...", "While some might argue otherwise, I believe..."
