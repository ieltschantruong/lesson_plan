2. Speaking Part 2 & 3: Practice

Overview, Vocab & paired-practice (65 mins)

Speaking Part 2: Individual Long Turn (PPF Strategy)

To speak continuously for 2 minutes without awkward pauses, structure your ideas chronologically:

  • Past (P): Recount when the event took place, establishing the initial setting.
  • Present (P): Focus on sensory details (what you saw, heard, broke, or used) in the actual moment.
  • Future (F): Reflect on how that moment changed your perspective, or whether you plan to repeat or avoid it.
Speaking Part 3: Deep Discussion (OREO Framework)

Avoid giving flat, short answers. Instead, structure your response logically:

  • Opinion (O): State your clear position immediately.
  • Reason (R): Explain *why* you believe this, using cause-and-effect connectors.
  • Example (E): Provide a concrete illustration or scenario.
  • Opinion Restated (O): Tie the thought back together to conclude.
01 Describe a time when the electricity suddenly went off

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: When it happened; Where you were; What you were doing; And explain how you handled the situation / how you felt.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd like to talk about a memorable power outage that occurred last summer. I was at home in my apartment, right in the middle of preparing for a crucial university final exam on my desktop computer. It was a scorching hot July evening, so I had the air conditioning running at full blast. Suddenly, without any warning, all the lights went out, and the hum of the AC died instantly. The entire neighborhood was plunged into pitch-black darkness. Initially, I panicked like a headless chicken because I hadn't saved my latest revision notes on the system. However, I soon realized panicking wouldn't help. I managed to locate my smartphone using its screen light and lit some scented candles. Since I couldn't study anymore, I went out onto the balcony to get some fresh air. Surprisingly, many of my neighbors had done the same. We ended up chatting, laughing, and sharing cold snacks under the starlight for a couple of hours. In the end, it was a blessing in disguise as it forced me to take a much-needed break and connect with my community."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Plunge into darkness (verb phrase)

Definition: To suddenly experience a total lack of light.
Example: The storm blew the local generator, plunging the entire street into pitch-black darkness.

2. Blessing in disguise (idiom)

Definition: Something bad that turned out to have a positive, unexpected outcome.
Example: Missing the flight was a blessing in disguise as I got to attend my close friend's party.

3. Like a headless chicken (idiom)

Definition: Acting in a panicked, highly disorganized and frantic manner.
Example: When the fire alarm went off, people started running around like headless chickens.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How dependent is modern society on electricity?
Ans (OREO): In my view, contemporary civilization is fundamentally dependent on electrical power to function. This is because all crucial infrastructures, including medical life-support systems, global banking, and communications, rely on stable grids. For example, a minor power failure in a metropolitan financial center immediately halts trades, causing billions of dollars in losses. Therefore, we are completely reliant on electricity, necessitating stronger backup structures.

02 Describe a time when you broke something

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What you broke; When/how it happened; How expensive/important it was; And explain how you felt and what you did afterward.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'm going to share an incident where I accidentally broke an irreplaceable family heirloom. This happened about two years ago during a spring cleaning session at my parents' house. I was dusting a high oak shelf in our living room when my elbow accidentally brushed against an antique porcelain vase that belonged to my late grandmother. I've always been a bit of a butterfingers, and in that split second, the vase slipped off the edge and shattered into pieces on the hardwood floor. It was of immense sentimental value to my mother, so my heart sank instantly and I felt completely mortified. However, instead of trying to sweep it under the rug, I decided to face the music. I carefully collected the fragments and went straight to my mother to apologize. While she was initially devastated, she appreciated my honesty. We later spent an afternoon using a special golden adhesive to piece it back together, creating a unique 'kintsugi' style vase that still holds a proud place in our home."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Shatter into pieces (verb phrase)

Definition: To break violently into many tiny, sharp fragments.
Example: The fragile crystal glass slipped from his hand and shattered into pieces on the stone patio.

2. Butterfingers (noun)

Definition: A person who habitually drops things or fails to catch them.
Example: Don't hand him that ancient porcelain, he is a total butterfingers!

3. Face the music (idiom)

Definition: To accept and confront the criticism, consequences, or punishment for your mistakes.
Example: After cheating on the test, he had to go to the principal's office and face the music.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why do some people prefer repairing old things rather than buying new ones?
Ans (OREO): In my view, individuals choose to restore old belongings because of deep sentimental attachments or eco-conscious values. This is due to the fact that vintage items often carry emotional memories that modern goods cannot replicate, while repairing items avoids landfill waste. For instance, my father rebuilt his grandfather's manual clock instead of getting a digital one because of pride and history. Therefore, fixing items honors the past and preserves resource sustainability.

03 Describe a piece of technology that you find difficult to use

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What piece of technology it is; What you use it for; Why you find it difficult to use; And explain how you feel about using it.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I would like to describe a piece of technology that presented a remarkably steep learning curve for me: a professional digital audio workstation, or DAW, called Pro Tools. I purchased this software about a year ago because I wanted to learn how to produce my own music and edit podcasts. However, upon opening it, I was immediately overwhelmed by its counterintuitive layout and the endless array of dials, sliders, and menus. Unlike basic editing apps, it felt completely user-unfriendly, and the detailed user manual was about as clear as mud. Every time I tried to import an audio track or route my microphone, the system would freeze or output no sound. I felt incredibly frustrated and ready to throw in the towel. Nevertheless, I decided to tackle the problem by watching structured online video tutorials. It took me months of daily trial and error to grasp the basic signal routing, but now I can navigate it with relative ease, though it still challenges me occasionally."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Steep learning curve (noun phrase)

Definition: Something that is exceptionally difficult to master or learn at the beginning.
Example: Coding in C++ has a steep learning curve, but it is deeply rewarding.

2. Counterintuitive layout (noun phrase)

Definition: Design structures that do not match natural expectations, logic, or user instinct.
Example: The app's counterintuitive layout made searching for simple files a complete headache.

3. Clear as mud (idiom)

Definition: Entirely confusing, ambiguous, or not clear at all.
Example: The directions the tour guide provided were about as clear as mud.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Do elder generations struggle to keep up with modern technology?
Ans (OREO): Yes, I indubitably believe seniors experience significant barriers adapting to digital advancements. This is primarily because software upgrades happen so rapidly, and touch-screen interfaces assume a baseline digital literacy that older people never had. For instance, my grandfather struggled immensely with online banking because the security authentications felt counterintuitive to his experience. Therefore, technology developers must create simpler, senior-friendly modes.

04 Describe a website that sells second-hand items

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What website it is; How you first discovered it; What products are sold there; And explain why you like or dislike using this website.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd like to talk about a second-hand marketplace website called Vinted, which specializes in pre-loved clothing and vintage accessories. I first stumbled upon this platform last year when I was looking to declutter my wardrobe and transition to a more sustainable lifestyle. The website operates as a peer-to-peer marketplace where users upload pictures of clothes they no longer wear. What I love about Vinted is the sheer thrill of the bargain hunt; you can find high-quality designer items that are virtually brand-new for dirt cheap—often a mere fraction of their original retail price. It's a steal compared to buying brand new items from high-end retail stores, which can easily cost an arm and a leg. The website's user interface is highly intuitive, allowing you to sort items by size, brand, and condition, making second-hand shopping a breeze."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Pre-loved clothing (noun phrase)

Definition: Second-hand clothes kept in good or like-new condition.
Example: Thrifting is a popular way to source pre-loved clothing for reasonable prices.

2. Dirt cheap / A steal (idiom)

Definition: Extremely inexpensive or offering great value.
Example: Getting a designer jacket for twenty dollars was an absolute steal.

3. Cost an arm and a leg (idiom)

Definition: Exorbitantly or heavily expensive.
Example: Buying luxury brand-new suits will easily cost you an arm and a leg.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why are second-hand platforms growing in popularity globally?
Ans (OREO): In my perspective, modern consumers favor second-hand sites because of environmental awareness and financial prudence. This is primarily because fast fashion generates massive industrial waste, prompting people to seek eco-friendly alternatives, while global inflation forces people to look for bargains. To illustrate, Gen Z shoppers are utilizing apps like Vinted to source vintage pieces instead of fast-fashion malls. Therefore, sustainable economics is driving the second-hand boom.

05 Describe a website you often visit

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: What website it is; What you do on this website; How you first heard of it; And explain why you find yourself visiting it frequently.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'm going to talk about Wikipedia, a website that I find myself visiting almost daily. It is a free online encyclopedia that I first discovered back in my primary school days when searching for research materials. I utilize this website for a wide array of purposes, ranging from verifying quick historical figures to keeping abreast of complex global scientific developments. What makes Wikipedia so appealing is its wealth of information coupled with its highly intuitive interface. You can search for virtually any obscure concept and find an extensive, well-referenced breakdown. I often find myself falling down internet rabbit holes, scrolling endlessly through hyperlinked articles for hours. Despite critics pointing out its open-source editing nature, the community's dedication to quality control is top-notch, making it an invaluable digital portal for me."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Intuitive interface (noun phrase)

Definition: User-friendly software design that is extremely easy to navigate without instructions.
Example: The website's intuitive interface allowed me to complete the registration in under a minute.

2. Keep abreast of (idiom)

Definition: To stay fully informed about the latest developments, news, or changes.
Example: It is crucial for doctors to keep abreast of modern medical research developments.

3. Internet rabbit holes (idiom)

Definition: Getting distracted online and spending hours looking up completely unrelated information.
Example: I searched for a simple recipe but fell down an internet rabbit hole about the history of spices.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Will online portals completely replace physical educational venues?
Ans (OREO): Unlikely. Although web platforms offer exceptional asynchronous learning structures, physical locations remain crucial for interactive development and socio-emotional growth. This is due to the fact that team project collaborations and social interaction require a physical environment. To illustrate, school students during virtual pandemic years suffered from severe loneliness and developmental delays. Therefore, blended learning represents the future of education, not outright replacement.
