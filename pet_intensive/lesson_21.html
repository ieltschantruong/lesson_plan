01 Describe an old person who has an interesting life and you enjoy talking to him/her

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Who this person is; How you know him/her; What they do/did; And explain why you think they have an interesting life and enjoy talking to them.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd love to tell you about an exceptional elderly gentleman named Mr. Lam, who resides next door to my family's house. He is a retired merchant marine officer in his late seventies. Over his decades-long maritime career, Mr. Lam literally traversed the globe, visiting nearly a hundred countries and collecting fascinating stories. I think he has an incredibly interesting life because his experiences aren't just limited to books; he survived fierce oceanic storms, navigated cultural differences in distant ports, and witnessed global historic transitions. I thoroughly enjoy talking to him because he is an absolute fountain of wisdom. Every weekend, we sit on his porch, and as he pours hot green tea, he reminisces about his voyages. His outlook on life is remarkably positive, and his broad worldviews always challenge my perspectives and offer me valuable life lessons."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Reminisce (verb) /ˌremɪˈnɪs/

Definition: To enjoy remembering and talking about past events or experiences.
Collocations: Reminisce fondly, reminisce about old times.
Example: We spent the entire evening reminiscing about our childhood adventures.

2. Globetrotter (noun) /ˈɡləʊbˌtrɒt.ər/

Definition: A person who travels widely and frequently all over the world.
Example: My grandfather was an avid globetrotter who collected stamps from every continent.

3. Fountain of wisdom (idiom)

Definition: A person who has a vast amount of knowledge, experience, and sound advice.
Example: In our community, elders are highly regarded as valuable fountains of wisdom.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why is it important for younger generations to maintain active communication with the elderly?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, fostering dialogue between youths and elders is paramount for maintaining cultural continuity. This is because the elderly possess invaluable historic insights and traditional knowledge that are absent from digital platforms. For instance, speaking to someone who lived through difficult socio-economic changes can offer youths deep contextual empathy. Therefore, active intergenerational communication bridges the generation gap and preserves cultural heritage.

02 Describe a good friend who is important to you

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Who this person is; How you met; What you do together; And explain why this person is important to you.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I want to share my thoughts on my dearest friend, Huy, who has been an indispensable part of my life for over a decade. We first crossed paths in middle school, sitting next to each other in mathematics class, and we hit it off immediately. Over the years, we've shared countless experiences, from preparing for university entrance exams to backpacking across rustic villages. Huy is incredibly important to me because he is my ultimate confidant and a kindred spirit. No matter what trials I face, he stands by me through thick and thin, offering objective advice and unyielding support. In our hyper-competitive world, finding someone who genuinely celebrates your successes and supports you in your lowest moments is rare. His constant presence acts as an anchor of emotional stability for me."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Confidant (noun) /ˈkɒn.fɪ.dænt/

Definition: A person with whom one shares a secret or private matter, trusting them not to tell others.
Collocations: Trusted confidant, close confidant.
Example: She needed a close confidant to talk through her difficult career dilemma.

2. Thick and thin (idiom)

Definition: Under all circumstances, no matter how difficult or challenging they are.
Example: Good friends stick with each other through thick and thin.

3. Kindred spirit (noun phrase)

Definition: A person whose interests, beliefs, or attitudes are similar to one's own.
Example: I immediately recognized him as a kindred spirit because of our shared love for classical literature.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: How has modern social media altered the core values of friendship in contemporary society?
Ans (OREO): In my view, the rise of digital platforms has unfortunately diluted the depth of interpersonal bonds. This is because people now prioritize superficial quantitative metrics, like followers and online interactions, over qualitative connections. For example, individuals often accumulate hundreds of virtual 'friends' but lack a single trusted confidant to rely on in a crisis. Therefore, while social media enhances global connectivity, it occasionally reduces actual psychological intimacy.

03 Describe a friend of yours who is good at music/singing

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: Who this friend is; How you know him/her; What musical talent they have; And explain how you feel about their talent.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd love to describe my talented high school classmate, Lan, who possesses an extraordinary gift for acoustic guitar and singing. We met during our school's freshman music club audition, and her performance completely blew me away. Lan is a true virtuoso; she has this uncanny ability to play intricate melodies by ear and holds a pitch-perfect singing voice that instantly captivates any audience. What I find most fascinating is how she can seamlessly blend folk and contemporary acoustic pop. I feel immensely proud and inspired by her musical talent. Whenever we gather for reunions, she brings her guitar and plays timeless acoustic covers. Watching her perform is deeply hypnotic and therapeutic. It's clear she puts her heart and soul into every chord, and I genuinely believe her talent has the potential to move audiences on a much grander stage."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Virtuoso (noun) /ˌvɜː.tʃuˈəʊ.səʊ/

Definition: A person highly skilled in music or another artistic pursuit.
Collocations: Guitar virtuoso, piano virtuoso.
Example: At just twelve, she was hailed as a violin virtuoso after her orchestral debut.

2. Pitch-perfect (adjective) /ˈpɪtʃˌpɜː.fɪkt/

Definition: Singing or playing in perfect tune; absolutely correct in pitch or representation.
Example: She gave a pitch-perfect rendition of the national anthem at the ceremony.

3. Strike a chord (idiom)

Definition: To create an emotional response or connect deeply with others.
Example: Her moving song about childhood memories struck a chord with the older audience.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Do you believe music education should be a compulsory part of secondary school curricula?
Ans (OREO): In my estimation, integrating music education into schools is exceptionally beneficial. This is because music cultivates neural pathways associated with mathematical comprehension, spatial awareness, and creative thinking. For example, studies show that students who play an instrument demonstrate higher logical-analytical skills. Therefore, music education is not a superficial hobby but a vital component of holistic academic development.

04 Describe a great dinner you and your friends or family members enjoyed

Part 2 Cue Card Bullets:

You should say: When and where you had this dinner; Who you enjoyed it with; What food you ate; And explain why you enjoyed this dinner so much.

Band 7.5+ Sample Response (Monologue):

"I'd like to share an experience of an exceptionally memorable celebratory dinner we had last autumn to mark my sister's graduation. We dined at a rustic, traditional garden restaurant on the outskirts of Hue. I enjoyed this feast with my immediate family and three of my sister's closest friends. The menu was an absolute culinary masterclass, featuring a vast assortment of local specialties, including mouth-watering roasted duck, steamed sea bass with lemongrass, and beautiful lotus-seed rice. What made this dinner so special was the exquisite atmospheric environment of the venue—open-air garden tables under soft lantern lighting. In our busy, separate routines, we rarely find time to slow down and connect. Sitting together, sharing delicious food, and listening to my sister reminisce about her college struggles created a profound sense of warmth and social cohesion. It was a perfect bonding moment that I'll cherish."

Lexical Masterclass (Detailed Vocab & Idioms):

1. Culinary (adjective) /ˈkʌl.ɪ.nər.i/

Definition: Of or relating to cooking or the kitchen.
Collocations: Culinary delights, culinary masterclass.
Example: The city of Hue is world-renowned for its rich, historic culinary arts.

2. Mouth-watering (adjective) /ˈmaʊθˌwɔː.tər.ɪŋ/

Definition: Smelling, looking, or sounding delicious, causing saliva to gather in the mouth.
Example: The bakery window was packed with a mouth-watering selection of pastries.

3. Break bread (idiom)

Definition: To share a meal with someone, often as a sign of friendship, reconciliation, or community.
Example: Gathering around the table to break bread is a wonderful way to ease interpersonal tensions.

Part 3 Analytical Extension:

Q: Why are communal family dinners considered essential for maintaining stable domestic relationships?
Ans (OREO): In my view, communal meals serve as a vital psychological anchor for family members. This is because shared dinners create a structured, screen-free space where members can engage in active listening and process daily struggles. For example, a child sharing their school experiences over dinner receives immediate emotional feedback. Therefore, regularly gathering to break bread is fundamental for reinforcing domestic cohesion.
