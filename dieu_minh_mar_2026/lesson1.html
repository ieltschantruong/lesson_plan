Part 1: Intro & Reality Check (00-15m)

1. The "Band 6.5" Mindset

  • Tell the student: "You do not need to be perfect to get a 6.5. In Listening and Reading, you can get 11 to 13 questions wrong and still score a 6.5. Our goal is to secure the 'easy' points in Parts 1 and 2, and survive the hard parts."
  • Tell the student: "In Speaking, a 6.0-6.5 means you can keep talking, even if you make some grammar mistakes. Fluency and willingness to speak are more important right now than perfect vocabulary."

2. CD-IELTS Interface Briefing

  • No 10-Minute Transfer: Remind them they only get 2 minutes at the very end of the Listening test to check answers. They must type their answers into the boxes while the audio is playing.
  • The "Tab" Key: Teach them to use the Tab key on the keyboard to jump to the next answer box quickly instead of using the mouse.

1. Strategy Explanation (10 mins)

Part 1: Predict & Trap (Form Completion)

  • Predicting: During the 30s reading time, look at blank spaces. Ask: Is this a name? Number? Date? Price? Noun?
  • Trap Words (Distractors): IELTS will give a "fake" answer before or after the real one. (e.g., 3:00 PM vs 3:30 PM).

Part 2: Synonyms & Direction (Multiple Choice/Maps)

  • Multiple Choice: Highlight keywords in the question, not just the answers. The audio will use synonyms for the answer choices.
  • Maps/Plans: Quickly find the starting point (e.g., "You are here") and trace the speaker's path using directional language (opposite, next to, beyond).

Part 3: Speaking & "A-R-E" (45-80m)

1. Concept Intro & Example (10 mins)

Explain Part 1 lasts 4-5 mins. Biggest mistake: 1-word answers. Teach the A-R-E method (2-3 sentences long):

A
Answer
Directly & immediately
R
Reason
Explain why
E
Example
Specific detail/story

Example: "Do you like your hometown?"

❌ Bad (Band 5): Yes, I do. It's nice.

✅ A-R-E (Band 6.5+): [A] Yes, I absolutely love living there. [R] It's a very peaceful place and the cost of living is quite low. [E] For example, I can get a huge bowl of Pho for just 30k VND near my house.

2. Live Practice Drill (25 mins)

Act as the examiner. If they give a short answer, stop them and say: "Give me the Reason. Give me the Example."

Topic Set A: Work and Study
  1. Do you work or are you a student?
  2. What do you like most about your job/studies?
  3. Is there anything you dislike about your daily routine?
  4. What would you like to do in the future?
Topic Set B: Hometown / Accommodation
  1. Let's talk about where you live. Do you live in a house or an apartment?
  2. What is your favorite room in your home? (Why?)
  3. What is the area around your home like?
  4. Do you plan to live there for a long time?
Topic Set C: Free Time (Hobbies)
  1. What do you usually do in your free time?
  2. Did you have different hobbies when you were a child?
  3. Is it important to have hobbies? (Why?)
💡
Feedback Focus:
  • Do not interrupt them while they speak.
  • Wait until they finish, then correct major grammar and praise A-R-E usage.
