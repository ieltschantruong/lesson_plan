1. Screen Navigation & Strategy (10 mins)

The Split-Screen Advantage

In CD-IELTS, the text is on the left, questions on the right. Teach them to highlight: They can right-click to highlight text or make notes on the screen.

Skimming vs. Scanning

  • Skimming: Read the title, subheadings, and the first sentence of each paragraph to get the main idea (1-2 mins max).
  • Scanning: Looking for specific keywords from the questions (Names, Dates, Capital letters, Numbers).

Common Question Types & Handling Tips

1. True / False / Not Given (T/F/NG)
  • True: The statement perfectly matches the text (usually with synonyms).
  • False: The statement contradicts the text. It says the exact opposite.
  • Not Given: You cannot find the information, or there isn't enough info to prove it true OR false. (Don't assume based on outside knowledge!)
2. Note / Table / Flow-chart Completion
  • Instruction Check: Always check the exact word limit (e.g., "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER").
  • Predict & Scan: Guess the missing word's grammatical type (noun, verb) and context. Scan the text for the surrounding keywords/synonyms.
3. Matching Headings / Information
  • Headings: Summarize the main idea. Read the first and last sentence of the paragraph carefully. Watch out for distractors that only mention a small detail.
  • Information: You are looking for a specific detail within a paragraph. Scan for synonyms of the keywords in the prompt.
4. Multiple Choice
  • Process of Elimination: Read the question stem, locate the relevant section in the text, and evaluate each option. Cross out options that are contradicted or Not Given.

Part 3: Speaking Part 2 (45-80m)

1. Concept & 1-Minute Prep (10 mins)

Explain Part 2: The "Long Turn". They get a cue card, exactly 1 minute to prepare notes, and must speak for 1 to 2 minutes.

❌ Note-taking MISTAKES

  • Writing full, complete sentences.
  • Writing a script to read from.
  • Writing down vocabulary words without context.

✅ Note-taking STRATEGY

  • Write only keywords and bullet points.
  • Use the WH-Question Framework: Who, What, When, Where, Why.
  • Alternatively, use Chronological Order: Beginning, Middle, End.

Major Speaking Part 2 Topics & Strategies

👤 A Person

Focus on appearance, personality traits (with anecdotes), and your relationship. Strategy: Use strong adjectives and relative clauses.

📍 A Place

Focus on location, physical description, atmosphere, and activities. Strategy: Use spatial language and sensory details (sight, sound).

📦 An Object / Thing

Focus on appearance, origin, function, and personal significance. Strategy: Practice describing utility and emotional attachment.

📅 An Event / Experience

Focus on chronological order (before, during, after) and your feelings. Strategy: Master narrative tenses (Past Simple, Past Continuous).

2. Live Practice Drill (25 mins)

For each card, set a timer for exactly 1 minute of silence for them to take notes. Then, set a timer for 2 minutes and have them speak. Do not interrupt them.

Cue Card 1: A Creative Person

Describe a creative person (e.g. an artist, a musician, an architect, etc.) you admire

You should say:

  • Who he/she is
  • How you knew him/her
  • What his/her greatest achievement is

And explain why you think he/she is creative.

Cue Card 2: An Exciting Activity

Describe an exciting activity you have tried for the first time

You should say:

  • What it is
  • When/where you did it
  • Why you thought it was exciting

And explain how you felt about it.

Cue Card 3: An Area of Science

Describe an area/subject of science (biology, robotics, etc.) that you are interested in and would like to learn more about

You should say:

  • Which area/subject it is
  • When and where you came to know this area/subject
  • How you get information about this area/subject

And explain why you are interested in this area/subject.

💡
Feedback Focus:
  • Did they speak for at least 1 minute and 30 seconds?
  • Did they look at their notes naturally, or were they just reading a script?
  • Praise them for using linking words (e.g., However, Therefore, To begin with).
