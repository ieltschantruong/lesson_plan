## **4-Week Lesson Plan (Computer-Delivered Focus)**

**Pedagogical Approach:**

* **Week 1:** Core Mechanics & CD-IELTS Familiarization  
* **Week 2:** Advanced Question Types & Methodologies  
* **Week 3:** Timed Practice & Integration  
* **Week 4:** Test Simulation & Weakness Targeting

**Format Adjustments for Vietnam (CD-IELTS):**

* *Listening:* Practice will enforce the strictly timed 2-minute review period at the end of the test (no 10-minute transfer time).  
* *Reading:* Practice should utilize screen-split layouts (text on left, questions on right) and emphasize using on-screen highlight/note tools.  
* *Writing:* Emphasize typing speed, utilizing the on-screen word count, and copy/paste editing strategies.  
* *Homework (HW):* Students should do all practice on a computer using official IDP/BC familiarization platforms or Cambridge CD-ROMs/online platforms, avoiding paper-based habits.

### **Week 1: Core Mechanics & Familiarization**

*Focus: Securing the "easy" points and establishing basic templates.*

**Lesson 1: Listening Foundations & Speaking Part 1**

* **00-15m:** Course overview, target setting (Band 6.0-6.5 realities), and Intro to CD-IELTS interface (drag-and-drop, highlighting).  
* **15-45m (Listening):** Strategies for Parts 1 & 2 (Form completion, short multiple choice). Focus on predicting answers and typing numbers/spelling quickly.  
* **45-80m (Speaking):** Part 1 overview. Expanding answers using the "A-R-E" (Answer, Reason, Example) method. Avoiding 1-word answers.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Complete one full Listening Part 1 & 2 section on a computer).

**Lesson 2: Reading Passage 1 & Speaking Part 2**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Listening Part 1 & 2 from Lesson 1).  
* **10-45m (Reading):** Skimming and scanning on a screen. Tackling Passage 1\. Focus on Note/Table completion and True/False/Not Given (T/F/NG).  
* **45-80m (Speaking):** Part 2 Long Turn. Note-taking strategies during the 1-minute prep. Structuring the 2-minute talk chronologically or by WH-questions.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Do one Reading Passage 1 online and record two Speaking Part 2 voice memos).

**Lesson 3: DEDICATED WRITING \- Task 1 (Dynamic Data)**

* **00-15m:** Writing module introduction, tutor expectations, and CD-IELTS typing interface overview. *(No cross-over with main tutor).*  
* **15-45m:** Task 1 overview. Analyzing Line Graphs and Bar Charts. Structuring the essay (Intro, Overview, Body 1, Body 2).  
* **45-75m:** Focus on the *Overview* paragraph—this is the deciding factor for a Band 6.0 in Task 1\. Vocabulary for trends (rose sharply, fluctuated).  
* **75-90m:** Typing drill & HW assignment (HW: Type one Task 1 Line Graph essay. Pay attention to typing speed and word count).

**Lesson 4: Reading Passage 2 & Listening Part 3**

* **00-15m:** HW Review (Feedback on Reading Passage 1 & listen to Speaking voice memos from Lesson 2).  
* **15-50m (Reading):** Passage 2 strategies. Tackling Matching Headings (drag and drop on CD-IELTS) and Matching Information. Identifying topic sentences.  
* **50-80m (Listening):** Tackling Part 3 (Long multiple-choice, matching). Managing the crucial reading time *before* the audio plays.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Complete Reading Passage 2 and Listening Part 3 online).

### **Week 2: Advanced Question Types & Methodologies**

*Focus: Pushing towards Band 6.5 by handling complex test features.*

**Lesson 5: Listening Part 4 & Speaking Part 3**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Review Reading and Listening from Lesson 4).  
* **10-45m (Listening):** Part 4 (Academic Monologue). Following signpost words. Maintaining focus during a 5-minute uninterrupted talk while typing simultaneously.  
* **45-80m (Speaking):** Part 3 (Abstract discussions). Using speculative language (*"It's highly likely that...", "One major consequence could be..."*). Comparing and contrasting.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Complete a full Listening test un-timed, focus on accuracy).

**Lesson 6: Reading Passage 3 & Integrated Speaking**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Review full Listening test from Lesson 5).  
* **10-50m (Reading):** Passage 3\. Tackling Yes/No/Not Given (Author's opinion) and Multiple Choice (Global vs. Detail). Dealing with unknown vocabulary contextually.  
* **50-80m (Speaking):** Full mock speaking interviews (Parts 1, 2, and 3 combined) with immediate feedback on fluency and vocabulary.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Complete one Reading Passage 2 & 3 un-timed).

**Lesson 7: DEDICATED WRITING \- Task 2 (Foundations & Body Paragraphs)**

* **00-15m:** HW Review (Detailed feedback on Task 1 Line Graph essay from Lesson 3).  
* **15-45m:** Task 2 essay structure (4-5 paragraphs). Analyzing prompt types (Opinion, Discussion, Problem/Solution). Focus on writing strong introductions and thesis statements.  
* **45-75m:** Body Paragraphs. Using the "PEEL" structure (Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link). *CD-IELTS Strategy:* Teach the student to type their core thesis and topic sentences first, then use enter/return to fill in the evidence.  
* **75-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Write a full Task 2 essay \- Opinion/Agree-Disagree. Strict 40-minute limit).

**Lesson 8: The "Half-Test" Stamina Builder (Receptive)**

* **00-15m:** HW Review (Analyze errors from Reading Passages 2 & 3 assigned in Lesson 6).  
* **15-50m (Reading Practice):** 35 Minutes timed practice: Passage 1 & 2 strictly on computer.  
* **50-65m (Reading Review):** Analyze errors. Why were mistakes made? (Vocabulary? Time? Misinterpretation?)  
* **65-85m (Listening Practice):** Timed practice: Sections 2 & 3\.  
* **85-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Review missed vocabulary from today's session).

### **Week 3: Timed Practice & Integration**

*Focus: Strict time management and full-section execution on screen.*

**Lesson 9: Listening Full Mock & Error Analysis**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Vocab from Lesson 8\) & Warm-up.  
* **10-40m (Listening Mock):** Full 30-minute Listening Test under strict exam conditions (using speakers to simulate test centers).  
* **40-45m (CD-IELTS Transfer):** Strict 2-minute checking time. No extra writing allowed.  
* **45-75m (Analysis):** Grade together. Deep dive into the transcript for missed questions. Identify "trap" words.  
* **75-90m (Speaking Polish):** Quick 15-minute drill on Speaking Part 2 fluency to break up the receptive heavy lesson.  
* **90m:** HW (HW: Do one full Reading test, strictly timed to 60 minutes on computer at home).

**Lesson 10: Reading Full Mock (Session Splitting)**

* **00-60m (Reading Mock):** Full 60-minute Reading Test under strict exam conditions. No dictionary, 1-minute warnings given by tutor.  
* **60-85m (Analysis):** Immediate grading. Focus review specifically on questions that differentiate a 5.5 from a 6.0/6.5 (usually T/F/NG and Matching).  
* **85-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Prepare vocabulary mind-maps for 3 common IELTS topics).

**Lesson 11: DEDICATED WRITING \- Task 1 (Static/Maps) & Task 2 (Discussion)**

* **00-15m:** HW Review (Detailed feedback on the Task 2 Opinion essay assigned in Lesson 7).  
* **15-50m (Task 1):** Maps and Processes. Vocabulary for locations, changes, and passive voice for processes. Recognizing the difference between dynamic and static charts.  
* **50-80m (Task 2):** "Discuss both views and give your opinion" essays. Live outlining on shared screen. Focus on grammatical range (complex sentences) for Band 6.5.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Write a full Task 2 Discussion essay).

**Lesson 12: Speaking Mocks & Reading Weakness Drill**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Discuss the vocabulary mind-maps from Lesson 10).  
* **10-45m (Speaking):** Two back-to-back full Speaking Mock Tests using different topics (e.g., Environment, then Technology).  
* **45-55m (Speaking Feedback):** Detailed feedback on grammatical range and lexical resource.  
* **55-80m (Reading Drill):** Drill the student's statistical worst question type based on Lesson 10 (e.g., consecutive passages of *just* Matching Headings).  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Listen to a 15-minute TED talk and summarize it verbally).

### **Week 4: Weakness Targeting & Test Readiness**

*Focus: Confidence building, final polishing, and CD-IELTS psychological readiness.*

**Lesson 13: Targeted Reading & Listening Rescue**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (Verbal summary of the TED talk from Lesson 12).  
* **10-45m (Reading):** Continued targeted drills. Focus on Time Management—practicing abandoning overly difficult questions to secure easier points and navigating the CD-IELTS UI efficiently.  
* **45-80m (Listening):** Drill the student's worst section. Practice shadowing the audio to catch fast speech.  
* **80-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Rest mind, light vocabulary review only).

**Lesson 14: Half-Test Simulation (Receptive Mix)**

* **00-40m (Reading):** Timed Passage 2 & 3 (40 mins).  
* **40-70m (Listening):** Timed Sections 1, 3, and 4 (30 mins).  
* **70-85m (Review):** Rapid grading and final strategic adjustments for receptive skills.  
* **85-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Review all notes from the past 3 weeks).

**Lesson 15: DEDICATED WRITING \- Full Mock & Live Marking**

* **00-15m:** HW Review (Detailed feedback on the Task 2 Discussion essay from Lesson 11).  
* **15-75m (Writing Mock):** Full 60-minute Writing Test (Task 1 & Task 2\) completed live on a shared document. Tutor observes typing habits, editing process, and pacing (ensuring student moves to Task 2 after 20 mins).  
* **75-85m (Live Marking):** Tutor marks the essays live on screen using the Band Descriptors. Point out exactly where the score jumps from 5.5 to 6.0/6.5.  
* **85-90m:** Wrap-up & HW (HW: Re-write the essays based on feedback).

**Lesson 16: Final Polish & Strategy Briefing**

* **00-10m:** HW Review (General check-in and review notes from Lesson 14).  
* **10-30m (Speaking):** Final, highly formal Speaking Mock Test.  
* **30-40m (Speaking Feedback):** Final notes on pronunciation and confidence.  
* **40-70m (Reading/Listening Final Tips):** Reviewing "guessing" strategies for when time runs out on the computer. How to handle panic if a listening section is missed.  
* **70-90m (Final Review):** Comprehensive feedback. CD-IELTS Test day logistics (sleep, ID requirements, eye fatigue from screen, check-in process in Vietnam). Final confidence boost.
