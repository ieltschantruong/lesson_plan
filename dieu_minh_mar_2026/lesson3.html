Lesson 3: DEDICATED WRITING

Task 1 (Dynamic Data) • 90 Minutes Total

Progress: 0 / 4

Goal

Introduce CD-IELTS Writing mechanics, master the Task 1 4-paragraph structure (Line/Bar graphs), and perfect the Band 6.0+ Overview paragraph.

Part 1: Intro & Interface (00-15m)

Expectations & CD-IELTS Mechanics

  • Separation of Concerns: Explain that you are their dedicated Writing tutor. You will only focus on Tasks 1 and 2, and will not review Reading/Listening/Speaking homework.
  • The Digital Advantage:
    • Word Count: The CD-IELTS interface has a live word count at the bottom of the screen. No more manual counting!
    • Copy/Paste: They can use standard keyboard shortcuts (Ctrl+C, Ctrl+V) to move sentences around. This fundamentally changes how they should edit their essays.
    • Legibility: Poor handwriting no longer penalizes their score.

Part 2: Task 1 Core Structure (15-45m)

Live Shared Document

Paste the link to your shared Google Doc (or similar platform) where the student will type their live exercises.

1. The 4-Paragraph Template (10 mins)

Teach the absolute standard template for Task 1. Do not let them deviate from this.

1. Introduction (1 sentence)

Paraphrase the prompt. What is the chart showing, where, and when?

2. Overview (2-3 sentences)

The most important paragraph! Identify the 2 or 3 main macro-trends. NO DATA/NUMBERS HERE.

3. Body Paragraph 1

Group the data logically. Compare the highest figures or the upward trends. Include specific numbers.

4. Body Paragraph 2

Group the remaining data. Compare the lowest figures or the downward/fluctuating trends. Include specific numbers.

2. Dynamic Data Analysis (20 mins)

  • Dynamic = Changes over time. Show them a Line Graph or a Bar Chart with years on the X-axis.
  • Ask them: "What is the first thing we look for?" (Answer: The overall upward or downward trajectory from the first year to the last year).
  • Grouping Data: Teach them not to describe every single line year-by-year. Group lines that behave similarly together.

Part 3: The Golden Overview (45-75m)

1. Why the Overview Matters (10 mins)

According to IELTS Band Descriptors: A Band 5.0 "recounts detail mechanically with no clear overview." A Band 6.0 "presents an overview with information appropriately selected."

Crucial Rule: Never put specific numbers, percentages, or exact dates in the Overview. Keep it general.

2. Trend Vocabulary Upgrade (10 mins)

Upward Trends 📈

Verbs: increased, rose, grew, surged, skyrocketed.

Nouns: an increase, a rise, a growth, an upward trend.

Downward Trends 📉

Verbs: decreased, fell, dropped, declined, plummeted.

Nouns: a decrease, a fall, a drop, a decline.

Adverbs & Adjectives (Degree of Change)

Big changes: significantly, dramatically, sharply, a significant rise.

Small/Steady changes: slightly, gradually, steadily, a gradual decline.

3. Live Overview Practice (10 mins)

ACTION

Provide a sample Line Graph to the student. Have them type an Overview paragraph in your shared document while you watch. Correct their vocabulary and ensure they use no specific numbers.

Part 4: Typing Drill & HW (75-90m)

1. Speed Typing Drill (10 mins)

Give the student a new prompt. Set a timer for 5 minutes. They must type the Introduction and Overview paragraphs in the shared doc before time runs out to test their CD-IELTS typing speed.

2. Assign Homework (5 mins)

📝 Task: Full Essay Type-Up

Write a complete Task 1 Line Graph or Bar Chart essay (minimum 150 words). Provide them with a specific prompt/image. They must type this in a word processor (Google Docs/Word) and pay attention to their word count.

⏰ Reminder

Inform them to send you the link/document 24 hours before your next dedicated writing session (Lesson 7 in the curriculum schedule) so you can prepare feedback.
