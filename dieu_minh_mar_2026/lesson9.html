Part 3: Speaking Part 2 Topics (55-80m)

Live Practice Drill

Give the student 1 minute of silence to prepare notes using the WH-question framework. Then, require them to speak for a full 2 minutes without interruption. Provide feedback on fluency and vocabulary after each attempt.

Cue Card 1: A Useful Book 1m prep + 2m speak

Describe a book you read that you found useful.

You should say:

  • What the book is
  • When you read it
  • What it is about

And explain why you found it useful.

💡 Brainstorming Ideas: A self-help book (like Atomic Habits or Rich Dad Poor Dad), a cooking recipe book, a travel guide for a past trip, or an IELTS preparation book.

Band 6.5 Sample Outline & Response

"I’d like to talk about a book that really changed my daily routine, called 'Atomic Habits' by James Clear. I read it about six months ago when I was feeling quite stressed and disorganized with my studies.

Basically, the book is about the psychology of habit formation. It explains how making tiny, 1-percent improvements every day can lead to massive changes over a year. The author shares practical strategies on how to build good habits, like exercising or studying, and how to break bad ones, like scrolling on social media too much.

I found this book incredibly useful because it gave me actionable advice, not just motivation. Before reading it, I used to procrastinate a lot with my IELTS preparation. However, by applying the 'two-minute rule' from the book—which means scaling down a new habit so it takes just two minutes to start—I managed to build a consistent daily study routine. It totally shifted my mindset and made a huge difference in my productivity."

Cue Card 2: A Helpful Person 1m prep + 2m speak

Describe a person who often helps others.

You should say:

  • Who this person is
  • How you know them
  • How they help others

And explain how you feel about this person.

💡 Brainstorming Ideas: A dedicated school teacher, a family member (like an aunt or mother) who cooks for the neighborhood, a friend who volunteers at an animal shelter, or a local charity organizer.

Band 6.5 Sample Outline & Response

"I'm going to describe my aunt, Lan, who is probably the most selfless and generous person I’ve ever met. I’ve known her my whole life, as she is my mother’s older sister, and she lives just a few blocks away from our family home.

She helps others in a variety of ways, but primarily through a local community kitchen she organizes. Every weekend, she gathers donations and cooks hundreds of meals for homeless people and struggling workers in the city. She doesn't just provide food; she also takes the time to sit and talk with them, offering emotional support and a listening ear. Furthermore, she's the kind of neighbor who is always willing to babysit kids or help out when someone is sick.

I feel profound respect and admiration for her. In today’s fast-paced world, where most people are only focused on their own careers and problems, it’s incredibly inspiring to see someone dedicate so much of their free time to making society a better place. She has definitely taught me the true meaning of compassion."
