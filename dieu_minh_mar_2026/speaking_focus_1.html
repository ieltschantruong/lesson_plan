Core Topics: Views, Childhood, Life Stages, Working Abroad

Student Task
Coaching / Pitfall Note
00:00 - 10:00 (10 mins)

Phase 1: Warm-up & Schema Activation

🗣️ Tutor Instructions

Your goal here is strictly to lower the affective filter. Do not correct any grammar during this phase. Smile, be encouraging, and introduce the topics casually. Ask them 2-3 easy questions to get their vocal cords moving.

  • "How are you feeling about the test today?"
  • "Before we start, tell me about a nice view you saw recently—maybe just out your window?"
  • "What was your favorite toy or game when you were 7 years old?"

⚠️ Coaching Tip for Weak Speakers

If they give one-word answers, gently prompt them with "Why is that?" or "Tell me a little more about that." Do not push too hard; just establish a rhythm.

10:00 - 25:00 (15 mins)

Phase 2: Targeted Vocabulary Learning

🗣️ Tutor Instructions

Introduce these 5 high-impact lexical items. Write them on a shared board or type them in the chat. Pronounce them clearly, emphasize the stress, and have the student repeat them twice.

Word / Phrase Meaning / Pronunciation Example Sentence
1. Breathtaking Extremely beautiful
/ˈbreθˌteɪkɪŋ/ (Stress on BRETH)		 "The view from the mountain was absolutely breathtaking."
2. Concrete jungle A city with too many buildings
Idiom		 "I prefer rural views because I get tired of the concrete jungle."
3. Nostalgic Happy but slightly sad remembering the past
/nɑːˈstældʒɪk/ (Stress on STAL)		 "Thinking about my childhood activities makes me feel very nostalgic."
4. Broaden my horizons To expand one's knowledge/experience
Collocation		 "Working in a foreign country is a great way to broaden my horizons."
5. Stepping stone Something that helps you advance
Idiom		 "A short-term job abroad is a good stepping stone for my career."

📝 Student Task: Fill-in-the-blank

Ask the student to complete these sentences verbally using the new vocabulary:

  • "When I look at old photos, I feel very _________."
  • "I don't like living in the city center; it's just a _________."
  • "I want to work in an international company to _________."
25:00 - 45:00 (20 mins)

Phase 3: Idea Brainstorming & Scaffolding

Weak speakers fail because they run out of ideas, not just words. Build a "bank" of ideas they can rely on during the mock.

Part 1 Strategy: A.R.E. (Answer, Reason, Example)

Practice this formula with the student using one question:

Q: Do you prefer views in urban areas or rural areas?
A (Answer): "I definitely prefer rural areas."
R (Reason): "Because the air is much fresher and it's less stressful."
E (Example): "For instance, looking at a green mountain is much better than looking at a concrete jungle."

Part 2 Strategy: Choosing a "Safe" Topic

The prompt is: Describe a short-term job you want to have in a foreign country. Weak speakers often freeze trying to think of a complex job. Guide them to choose a simple, easily describable job right now.

Ask the student: "Which of these 3 sounds easiest to talk about? Let's use that one for the mock."

Part 3 Brainstorm: Pros and Cons

Help the student list bullet points so they aren't surprised by the abstract questions.

Challenges of working abroad:
  • Language barrier
  • Homesickness / missing family
  • Culture shock
Benefits of working abroad:
  • Broaden horizons
  • Learn independence
  • Good for the CV / Stepping stone
45:00 - 60:00 (15 mins)

Phase 4: Full Timed Mock Test

⏱️ Tutor Instructions: Strict Examiner Mode

Tell the student: "I am now going to act as the examiner. I will not smile as much, and I will not help you or correct you. This is to simulate real test pressure. Do your best, keep talking."

Take silent notes on a piece of paper. DO NOT INTERRUPT.

Part 1 (4-5 minutes)

Views

  • Do you like taking pictures of different views?
  • Do you prefer views in urban areas or rural areas?
  • Have you seen an unforgettable and beautiful view?

Childhood & Life Stages

  • What were your favorite activities when you were a child?
  • Do you enjoy being the age you are now?
  • At what age do you think people are the happiest?

Part 2 (1 min prep, 2 mins speaking)

"I will give you 1 minute to prepare, and then you will speak for 1 to 2 minutes."

Describe a short-term job you want to have in a foreign country

You should say:

  • Where it is
  • How you know of it
  • What the job is
  • And explain why you want to do it.

Part 3 (4-5 minutes)

  • What short-term jobs do young people do in other countries?
  • What challenges do young people face when working abroad?
  • What are the benefits of working for an international company?
  • Why are some people unwilling to work in other countries?
60:00 - 75:00 (15 mins)

Phase 5: Feedback & Error Correction

🗣️ Tutor Instructions: The Feedback Protocol

Break character and smile. Ask them how they felt it went. Then provide structured feedback using the Sandwich Method.

1. Positive (Fluency)

"I loved how you kept talking even when you weren't sure of the word. Your fluency in Part 2 was much better because you picked an easy job!"

2. Constructive (Focus on 2 things max)

"I noticed you struggled with past tense in the childhood questions. You said 'I play football'. It should be 'I played'. Also, let's look at one vocabulary upgrade..."

3. Positive (Vocabulary)

"But you successfully used 'broaden my horizons' in Part 3! That is an automatic score booster. Excellent job."

📝 Student Task: Immediate Re-do

Pick one question they answered poorly during the mock test. Ask them to answer it again right now, applying your corrections. This builds immediate muscle memory.

75:00 - 90:00 (15 mins)

Phase 6: Closing Drilling & Repetition

🗣️ Tutor Instructions: Fluency Drills

To solidify their confidence and vocabulary, run a quick "shadowing" drill. You say the phrase naturally, and the student repeats it immediately matching your intonation.

  • "When I was a child, I used to..." (Student repeats)
  • "...play outside with my friends." (Student repeats)
  • "Working abroad is a great stepping stone..." (Student repeats)
  • "...because it helps me broaden my horizons." (Student repeats)

🏁 Closing the Session

Remind the student of their progress today. Assign homework: Ask them to record a voice memo on their phone answering the Part 2 question again using the notes they took today, and send it to you before the next session.

💡 Part 1: Ideas & Responses

Topic: Views

Q: Do you like taking pictures of different views?

Ideas: Keep memories, share on social media, appreciate nature.

Sample: "Yes, I really enjoy it. Taking photos helps me keep a record of beautiful places I visit, and I like sharing them on my Instagram with my friends."

Q: Do you prefer views in urban areas or rural areas?

Ideas: Fresh air, relaxing vs. concrete jungle, noise.

Sample: "I definitely prefer rural areas. The air is much fresher and it's less stressful. Looking at a green mountain is much better than looking at a concrete jungle."

Q: Do you prefer views in your own country or in other countries?

Ideas: Exotic, different architecture, broaden horizons.

Sample: "I prefer views in other countries because they are completely different from what I'm used to. Experiencing new scenery helps me broaden my horizons."

Q: Have you seen an unforgettable and beautiful view or scenery?

Ideas: Beach sunset, mountain top, specific trip.

Sample: "Yes, last year I visited a coastal city. The view of the sunset over the ocean was absolutely breathtaking and very memorable."

Topic: Childhood Activities

Q: What are your favorite activities?

Ideas: Relaxing, sports, reading, watching movies.

Sample: "Right now, I enjoy reading and jogging. These activities really help me unwind and reduce stress after a long day at work."

Q: What were your favorite activities when you were a child?

Ideas: Outdoor games, toys, hide-and-seek.

Sample: "When I was young, I loved playing hide-and-seek outside with my neighbors. Thinking about it makes me feel quite nostalgic."

Q: Did you prefer to do activities alone or with a group of people when you were a child?

Ideas: Extrovert, teamwork, more fun.

Sample: "I definitely preferred group activities. Playing with friends was much more exciting and fun than just playing with toys by myself."

Q: Are there any differences between the activities you liked when you were a child and those you like now?

Ideas: Active vs. passive, physical energy, busy life.

Sample: "Yes, a huge difference. As a kid, I loved running around and being loud. But now, because my job is tiring, I prefer quiet and passive activities like reading."

Topic: Life Stages

Q: Do you enjoy being the age you are now?

Ideas: Independence, maturity, financial freedom vs. stress.

Sample: "Yes, being in my twenties is great because I have more freedom and independence, even though work can sometimes be stressful."

Q: What did you often do with your friends in your childhood?

Ideas: Video games, school sports, exploring.

Sample: "We usually played football in the park right after school. We would stay outside until it got dark."

Q: What do you think is the most important at the moment?

Ideas: Career, exams, health, saving money.

Sample: "Right now, passing this exam and focusing on my career is the most important thing. I'm trying to find a good stepping stone for my future."

Q: Do you have any plans for the next five years?

Ideas: Study abroad, get promoted, buy a house.

Sample: "In the next five years, I hope to get a master's degree and perhaps work abroad to gain more international experience."

Q: How do people remember each stage of their lives?

Ideas: Photos, diaries, social media, talking to old friends.

Sample: "People usually look back at old photographs or videos on their phones. It makes them feel very nostalgic about the past."

Q: At what age do you think people are the happiest?

Ideas: Childhood (no stress) OR retirement (free time).

Sample: "I think childhood is the happiest time because kids have no responsibilities, no bills to pay, and no financial stress."

💡 Part 2: Structure & Sample Monologue

Prompt: Describe a short-term job you want to have in a foreign country

Focus on the Barista in Australia template. It's easy, relies on everyday vocabulary, and fills the time nicely.

1. Where it is

"I'd like to talk about a short-term job I’d love to do, which is working as a barista in a coffee shop in Melbourne, Australia."

2. How you know of it

"I know about this opportunity because a senior from my university did a working holiday there last year. She posted some beautiful, breathtaking pictures on Instagram, which made me really curious."

3. What the job is

"The job simply involves making coffee, taking orders, and chatting with local customers. It’s a fast-paced environment, but it seems really fun and social."

4. And explain why you want to do it

"I want to do this for a few reasons. First, it would force me to speak English all day, which is a great way to improve. Second, earning some local money would help me travel around the country. Finally, I think living abroad, even just for a few months, is an excellent way to broaden my horizons and would serve as a good stepping stone for my future career."

💡 Part 3: Ideas & Responses

Q: What short-term jobs do young people do in other countries?

Ideas: Hospitality (waiter/barista), agriculture (fruit picking), tourism (tour guide).

Sample: "Many young people work in the service industry, like as waiters or baristas. Others might do seasonal farm work, such as fruit picking, because these jobs usually don't require high qualifications."

Q: What challenges do young people face when working abroad?

Ideas: Language barrier, culture shock, homesickness, finding housing.

Sample: "The biggest challenge is definitely the language barrier. Besides that, they often suffer from homesickness since they are far away from their family and friends in a completely new culture."

Q: What are the benefits of working for an international company?

Ideas: High salary, professional environment, global network, resume building.

Sample: "It offers a very professional working environment and a chance to build a global network. Having an international company on your resume is also a great stepping stone for future jobs."

Q: What personal skills are required to work in an international company?

Ideas: Communication, adaptability, teamwork, open-mindedness.

Sample: "You definitely need strong communication skills, especially in English. Furthermore, the ability to adapt to different working styles and cultures is crucial for teamwork."

Q: What kind of work can young people do in foreign countries?

Ideas: Paid internships, teaching languages, remote freelance work.

Sample: "Besides basic manual labor, they can also do paid internships related to their university major, or they can work as language teachers if they are fluent in English."

Q: Why are some people unwilling to work in other countries?

Ideas: Fear of the unknown, comfort zone, family ties, language anxiety.

Sample: "Some people are simply afraid of leaving their comfort zone. They might also have strong family ties and don't want to live far away, or they lack confidence in their foreign language skills."
