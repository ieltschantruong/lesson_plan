Phase 1: Warm-up & Schema Activation

🗣️ Tutor Instructions

Lower the affective filter. Do not correct grammar yet. Smile and introduce today's themes casually to get their vocal cords moving.

🗣️ Tutor Instructions

Introduce these 5 high-impact lexical items for today's topics. Have the student listen to the pronunciation and repeat them twice.

Word / Phrase Meaning / Pronunciation Example Sentence

📝 Student Task: Fill-in-the-blank

Ask the student to complete these sentences verbally using the new vocabulary:

  • "When I need to relax, I usually _________ around my neighborhood."
  • "I took a photo of the ocean because it was so _________."
  • "She didn't know which job to accept; she was in a real _________."
25:00 - 45:00 (20 mins)

Phase 3: Idea Brainstorming & Scaffolding

Let's build a new "bank" of ideas for today's specific topics so the student doesn't freeze during the mock.

Part 1 Strategy: Reinforce A.R.E.

Practice the A.R.E. formula (Answer, Reason, Example) using a Scenery question:

Q: Do you prefer the mountains or the sea?
A (Answer): "I definitely prefer the sea."
R (Reason): "Because listening to the waves is very relaxing for me."
E (Example): "For example, whenever I feel stressed, taking a stroll on the beach completely clears my mind."

Part 2 Strategy: Choosing a "Safe" Story

The prompt is: Describe a time when you gave advice to others. Weak speakers often try to invent a complex moral dilemma. Guide them to a simple, everyday scenario.

Ask the student: "Which of these 3 stories sounds easiest for you to tell right now?"

Part 3 Brainstorm: Abstract Concepts

Help the student contrast ideas for Part 3. Let's map out "Online Strangers vs. Friends/Family" for advice.

Asking Strangers Online:
  • Unbiased / Objective opinions
  • Can keep it anonymous
  • Risk of trolls or fake experts
Asking Friends/Parents:
  • They know your background
  • More emotional support
  • Might be biased / overprotective

⏱️ Tutor Instructions: Strict Examiner Mode

Tell the student: "I am now the examiner. I will not help you or correct you. Do your best, keep talking."

60:00 - 75:00 (15 mins)

Phase 5: Feedback & Error Correction

🗣️ Tutor Instructions: Sandwich Feedback

Break character and smile. Use the Sandwich Method from Session 1, referencing today's performance.

1. Positive (Fluency/Idea)

"I noticed you didn't hesitate as much during Part 1 today! You had great ideas for the 'Walking' questions."

2. Constructive (Fix max 2 errors)

"In Part 2, when talking about advice, watch out for the word 'advice'—it is uncountable. We say 'some advice', not 'an advice' or 'advices'."

Pick one question they stumbled on during the mock. Ask them to answer it again right now with the corrections in mind.

Phase 6: Closing Drilling & Repetition

🗣️ Tutor Instructions: Fluency Drills

Run a quick "shadowing" drill. You say the phrase naturally, and the student repeats it immediately matching your intonation.

Assign homework: Ask them to record a voice memo answering the Part 2 "Advice" question again, making sure to use the word "dilemma" and paying attention to uncountable noun rules. Send before the next session.

💡 Part 1: Ideas & Responses

Topic: Building

Q: Are there tall buildings near your home?

Ideas: City center vs suburbs, apartments, modern vs old.

Sample: "Yes, quite a few. I live near the city center, so there are many modern skyscrapers and high-rise apartment blocks right next to my neighborhood."

Q: Do you take photos of buildings?

Ideas: Architecture, historical places, travel.

Sample: "Yes, especially when I travel. I love taking pictures of historical buildings because the architecture is often very unique and picturesque."

Q: Is there a building that you would like to visit?

Ideas: Famous landmarks (Burj Khalifa, Eiffel Tower, local museum).

Sample: "I've always wanted to visit the Burj Khalifa in Dubai. It's the tallest skyscraper in the world, and I imagine the view from the top is breathtaking."

Topic: Scenery

Q: Do you look out the window at the scenery when traveling by bus or car?

Ideas: Avoid motion sickness, enjoy nature, relax vs sleep/phone.

Sample: "Yes, absolutely. Looking at the passing scenery helps me relax and prevents me from getting carsick. It's much better than staring at my phone."

Q: Do you prefer the mountains or the sea?

Ideas: Hiking, fresh air VS swimming, waves, relaxing.

Sample: "I definitely prefer the sea. Listening to the sound of the waves is very soothing for me. Whenever I'm stressed, a walk on the beach clears my mind."

Q: Do you like to take scenery pictures?

Ideas: Capturing memories, nature photography, sharing online.

Sample: "Yes, I take lots of them. Whenever I see a beautiful sunset or a picturesque landscape, I use my smartphone to capture the moment to share with my friends."

Q: What are the most beautiful sights you have seen while traveling?

Ideas: Snow, beaches, forests, specific trips.

Sample: "The most beautiful sight I've seen was a snow-covered mountain during a trip last winter. The white snow against the blue sky was completely stunning."

Topic: Walking

Q: Do you walk a lot?

Ideas: Health, commute, busy lifestyle.

Sample: "Yes, I try to walk every day. I usually walk from the train station to my office, which is a great way to get some light exercise into my routine."

Q: Did you often go outside to have a walk when you were a child?

Ideas: Playing with friends, going to the park, safe neighborhoods.

Sample: "Yes, all the time. When I was younger, I used to walk to the local park every afternoon to play games with my friends until dinner time."

Q: Why do people like to walk in parks?

Ideas: Nature, escape the city, safe from cars, exercise.

Sample: "People like it because parks offer an escape from the concrete jungle. It's a safe place away from traffic where you can breathe fresh air and enjoy nature."

Q: Where would you like to take a long walk if you had the chance?

Ideas: Beach, forest trail, famous hiking spot.

Sample: "I would love to take a long walk along a quiet, sandy beach at sunset. I think the sound of the ocean and the beautiful colors would be amazing."

Q: Where did you go for a walk lately?

Ideas: Neighborhood, local park, shopping mall.

Sample: "Just last night, I took a short stroll around my neighborhood after dinner. It helps me digest my food and wind down before going to sleep."

💡 Part 2: Structure & Sample Monologue

Prompt: Describe a time when you gave advice to others

Focus on the University Major template. It's a relatable story, relies on common vocabulary, and fits the time requirement easily.

1. When it was

"I'd like to talk about a time I gave some important advice. This happened about a year ago, during the summer holiday right before the university semester started."

2. To whom you gave the advice

"I gave this advice to my younger cousin. He had just graduated from high school and was in a real dilemma about what to study at university."

3. What the advice was

"His parents wanted him to study Finance to make money, but he loved Art. I told him some words of wisdom: I advised him to follow his passion and study Graphic Design. I explained that it’s a modern field that combines art with good job prospects."

4. And explain why you gave the advice

"I gave him this advice because I believe you can only be truly successful if you enjoy what you do every day. If he chose Finance, he would be miserable. Ultimately, he listened to me, and now he is doing really well in his design classes and feels very happy."

💡 Part 3: Ideas & Responses

Q: Should people prepare before giving advice?

Ideas: Yes, to avoid bad advice, understand the context, think logically.

Sample: "Yes, definitely. If it's a serious dilemma, you should take time to understand the person's situation fully. Giving rushed advice might accidentally cause more problems for them."

Q: Is it good to ask advice from strangers online?

Ideas: Pros: Unbiased, anonymous. Cons: Trolls, lack of context, fake info.

Sample: "It has pros and cons. It's good because strangers are objective and unbiased. However, it can be risky because they don't know your personal background, and some people online might just give terrible or fake advice."

Q: What are the personalities of people whose job is to give advice to others?

Ideas: Therapists, counselors -> Patient, empathetic, good listeners.

Sample: "Professionals like therapists or counselors are usually very empathetic and patient. Most importantly, they are excellent listeners who can analyze a situation without judging the person."

Q: What are the problems if you ask too many people for advice?

Ideas: Confusion, conflicting opinions, unable to decide.

Sample: "If you ask too many people, you will get too many conflicting opinions. This will just make you feel more confused and stressed, making it harder to make a final decision."

Q: Why do some people think it is better to ask for advice from friends than from parents?

Ideas: Generation gap, friends relate better, less judgment.

Sample: "Friends are usually the same age, so they understand modern problems better than parents do. Parents might have a generation gap, or they might be too strict and overprotective."

Q: When would old people ask young people for advice?

Ideas: Technology, smartphones, modern trends, internet.

Sample: "The most common situation is regarding technology. Older people often ask young people for help on how to use new smartphones, download apps, or navigate social media platforms."
