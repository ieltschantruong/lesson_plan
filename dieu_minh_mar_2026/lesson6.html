1. Strategy: Tackling the Hardest Passage (10 mins)

Yes / No / Not Given (Author's Opinion)

Unlike T/F/NG (which is about factual information), Y/N/NG is about the claims or views of the writer. The student must look for opinion words in the text (e.g., argues, suggests, believes, unfortunately, surprisingly).

Global Multiple Choice vs. Detail Multiple Choice

  • Detail: "In paragraph 3, what does the writer say about X?" → Go straight to paragraph 3.
  • Global: "What is the main purpose of this article?" or "What is the best title for this text?" → Leave this question until the very end! Do not answer it first.

Part 1: General Questions (4-5 mins)

Topic 1: Street Market

  1. What do people usually buy at the street market?
  2. Do you prefer to go shopping in the shopping mall or at the street market?
  3. When was the last time you went to a street market?
  4. Are there many street markets in Vietnam?

💡 Ideas: Fresh produce, bargaining, bustling atmosphere, traditional food.

✅ Q2 Sample (A-R-E): "I generally prefer shopping malls. The main reason is comfort, as they are air-conditioned and well-organized. For example, during the hot summers in Vietnam, it's much more relaxing to walk around a cool mall than a crowded, outdoor street market."

Topic 2: Happiness

  1. Do you prefer to watch happy or sad movies?
  2. Is there anything that has made you feel happy lately?
  3. What made you happy when you were little?
  4. What do you think will make you feel happy in the future?

💡 Ideas: Unwinding, stress relief, achieving goals, simple childhood games.

✅ Q1 Sample (A-R-E): "I definitely prefer watching happy movies, like comedies. Since I have a pretty stressful job, I want to unwind and laugh when I watch TV. For instance, I usually watch sitcoms on the weekends just to clear my mind and relax."

Topic 3: Crowded Place

  1. Is the city where you live crowded?
  2. Is there a crowded place near where you live?
  3. Do you like crowded places?
  4. Do most people like crowded places?
  5. When was the last time you were in a crowded place?

💡 Ideas: Traffic jams, feeling overwhelmed, lively festivals, claustrophobia.

✅ Q3 Sample (A-R-E): "To be honest, I don't really like crowded places. I find them quite overwhelming and noisy. For example, I try to avoid going to supermarkets on weekends because the huge crowds make me feel stressed and claustrophobic."

Part 2: Long Turn (3-4 mins) 1 min prep + 2 min speak

Describe an occasion that you saw a lot of people smiling.

You should say:

  • When and where this happened
  • Who you were with
  • What exactly was happening

And explain why so many people were smiling.

Tutor Note: Remind them to use chronological order or the WH-question framework for their 1-minute notes. They must speak for at least 1:30.
Band 6.5+ Sample Response

"I'd like to talk about a specific occasion when I saw a huge crowd of people smiling and laughing. This happened just last year during the Lunar New Year festival, which we call Tet in Vietnam. I was at the central square in my hometown, waiting for the midnight fireworks display.

I went there with my entire family, including my parents and my younger sister. What was happening was the city had organized a massive countdown event. There was live music, colorful decorations everywhere, and thousands of locals gathered together in the cool night air.

I think so many people were smiling for a few reasons. First, Tet is the most important holiday in our culture, representing a fresh start and hope for the new year. All the stress from the previous year was temporarily gone. When the fireworks finally went off at exactly midnight, lighting up the sky, I looked around and saw genuine joy on everyone's faces. People were hugging, taking photos, and wishing strangers a happy new year. It was a contagious feeling of community and optimism, which is why that memory stands out to me so clearly."

Part 3: Abstract Discussion (4-5 mins)

"We've been talking about seeing people smile, and I'd now like to ask you some general questions related to this."

1. Why do you think people smile when they meet someone for the first time?

Idea: Politeness, breaking the ice, showing you are approachable and not a threat.

Band 6.5 Sample

"I suppose it's primarily a matter of politeness and social etiquette. Smiling is a universal way to break the ice and show the other person that you are friendly and approachable. For instance, in a job interview or on a first date, a warm smile helps to reduce tension and build a quick connection."

2. Do you think people in your country smile more than people in other countries? Why or why not?

Idea: Cultural differences. E.g., Vietnamese people often smile to hide embarrassment or to show hospitality.

Band 6.5 Sample

"It's hard to generalize, but I do think people in my country smile quite a lot. In our culture, hospitality is highly valued, so locals naturally smile to make tourists or guests feel welcome. Interestingly, we also sometimes smile when we feel awkward or embarrassed as a way to "save face," which might be quite different from Western cultures."

3. How can smiling affect a person's mood or the mood of people around them?

Idea: Contagious emotions, scientifically triggering endorphins in the brain.

Band 6.5 Sample

"Well, scientifically speaking, smiling can actually trick your brain into feeling happier by releasing positive hormones like endorphins. Furthermore, emotions are highly contagious. If a teacher walks into a classroom with a big smile, it almost instantly lifts the energy of the room and makes the students feel more relaxed and positive."

4. In what kinds of situations is it considered inappropriate to smile?

Idea: Funerals, serious business negotiations, when someone is giving you bad news or a scolding.

Band 6.5 Sample

"There are certainly times when smiling is completely inappropriate. The most obvious example would be at a funeral or when someone is sharing sad news with you, as smiling would seem disrespectful and unempathetic. Additionally, in a very strict professional setting, like a serious business negotiation, smiling too much might make you look unprofessional or naive."

5. Do you agree that a fake smile is easy to recognize? How can you tell?

Idea: Genuine smiles reach the eyes (crinkling the corners), while fake smiles only use the mouth.

Band 6.5 Sample

"Yes, I completely agree. It's usually quite easy to spot a fake smile if you pay attention to the person's eyes. A genuine smile, often called a Duchenne smile, causes the skin around the eyes to crinkle. A fake smile, on the other hand, usually only involves the mouth, so the person's eyes look cold or emotionally disconnected."
