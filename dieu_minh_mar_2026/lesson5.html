1. Strategy: Surviving the Monologue (10 mins)

The Part 4 Reality

Part 4 is a 5-minute academic lecture. There is no break in the middle. The biggest challenge is losing your place and panicking.

Tracking "Signpost Words"

The speaker will guide you through the bullet points using signposts. Teach the student to listen for:

  • Introductions: "Today I'd like to talk about...", "Let's start by looking at..."
  • Moving to a new bullet point/section: "Now, let's turn to...", "Moving on to...", "Another significant factor is..."
  • Contrasts (Traps!): "However...", "On the other hand...", "Despite this..."

The "Move On" Rule

If you miss an answer, let it go immediately. If you think about question 34 too long, you will miss 35, 36, and 37.

Part 3: Speaking Part 3 (45-80m)

1. Concept: Abstract Discussion (10 mins)

Explain that Part 3 is an abstract discussion related to the Part 2 topic. It is NOT about them personally anymore. It is about society, the world, the past, and the future.

❌ Band 5.0 Mistake

Making it personal.

Q: Do people read more now than in the past?
A: Well, I don't read much because I am busy with homework...

✅ Band 6.5+ Strategy

Speaking generally using Speculative Language.

A: Generally speaking, I think society reads less books now. It's highly likely that smartphones have replaced traditional reading habits for most young people.

Useful "Buying Time" Phrases

  • "That's a very interesting question..."
  • "I've never really thought about it before, but I suppose..."
  • "Well, there are a few ways to look at this..."

2. Live Practice Drill (25 mins)

Topic Theme: Happiness, Crowds, and Social Events (Linked to: "An occasion you saw a lot of people smiling"). Ask the questions below. Force them to give multi-sentence answers using the A-R-E framework.

Discussion Questions

Q1: Why do you think people generally smile when they are taking photos?

💡 Ideas: Social expectations, creating lasting positive memories, projecting a good image on social media.

Band 6.5 Sample

"Generally speaking, I think it's a social expectation. When people look back at old photos, they want to remember the good times, not the stressful ones. For example, even if someone is having a bad day, they will usually force a smile for the camera so that their Instagram followers think they are enjoying themselves."

Q2: Is it important to show a positive facial expression in customer service jobs? Why or why not?

💡 Ideas: Makes customers feel welcome, reflects well on the company brand, helps diffuse angry situations.

Band 6.5 Sample

"Yes, it is absolutely crucial. A warm smile makes customers feel welcomed and valued, which is good for business. If a shop assistant looks angry or bored, customers might leave a bad review online or simply never return to that store again."

Q3: Do you think people in your country are generally happier now than they were in the past?

💡 Ideas: Better living standards and economy VS higher stress, longer work hours, and modern pressure.

Band 6.5 Sample

"That's a complex question. On the one hand, our living standards have improved significantly, which makes daily life easier. However, I believe modern life is much more stressful. People today work longer hours and face a lot of peer pressure from social media, which probably makes them less happy overall."

Q4: How does being in a crowd of happy people (like at a concert or festival) affect an individual's mood?

💡 Ideas: Contagious emotions, feeling a sense of belonging, escaping the daily routine.

Band 6.5 Sample

"I suppose emotions are highly contagious. When you are surrounded by thousands of cheering people at a music festival, it's almost impossible to stay sad. The collective energy lifts your mood and helps you forget about your daily worries, at least for a few hours."
