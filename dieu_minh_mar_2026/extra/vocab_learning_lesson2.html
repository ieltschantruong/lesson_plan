Innovative
Adjective /ˈɪnəveɪtɪv/
Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: Introducing new ideas; original and creative in thinking.

Vietnamese: Sáng tạo, mang tính đổi mới.

Usage: Often used to describe ideas, methods, or people who introduce fresh approaches rather than following the norm.

Example"I admire him because his approach to architecture is incredibly innovative."
Visionary
Noun/Adj /ˈvɪʒənri/
Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: Thinking about or planning the future with imagination or wisdom.

Vietnamese: Người nhìn xa trông rộng / Có tầm nhìn.

Usage: Used to praise leaders or creators who can anticipate future trends and possibilities.

Example"She is a true visionary in the tech industry, always anticipating trends."
Masterpiece
Noun /ˈmɑːstəpiːs/
Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: A work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship.

Vietnamese: Kiệt tác.

Example"Her greatest achievement was composing a symphony that critics called a masterpiece."

Topic: A Creative Person

Vietnamese: Khác thường, độc đáo, không theo quy chuẩn.

Topic: A Creative Person

Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: The process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something.

Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: New and unusual or experimental ideas, especially in the arts.

Vietnamese: Tiên phong, đi trước thời đại.

Think outside the box

Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: To think creatively, freely, and off the beaten path.

Vietnamese: Suy nghĩ đột phá, thoát khỏi khuôn mẫu.

Usage: A popular idiom used in professional and creative contexts to encourage original, non-traditional problem-solving.

Example"His ability to think outside the box allows him to solve problems uniquely."

Topic: A Creative Person

Definition: Very great or intense; having or showing great knowledge or insight.

Vietnamese: Sâu sắc, uyên thâm.

Usage: Usually collocated with abstract nouns like "impact", "effect", or "knowledge" to describe something deeply significant.

Topic: An Exciting Activity

Vietnamese: Cảm giác hưng phấn tột độ.

Complex

Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: Consisting of many different and connected parts; not easy to understand.

Vietnamese: Phức tạp.

Usage: Describes systems, theories, or mathematical problems that are intricate rather than simple.

Example"Genetics is a highly complex subject, which is why I find it stimulating."

Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: The practical use or relevance of something (such as a theory or discovery).

Vietnamese: Ứng dụng thực tiễn.

Curiosity
Noun /ˌkjʊəriˈɒsɪti/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: A strong desire to know or learn something.

Vietnamese: Sự tò mò, ham học hỏi.

Usage: Often collocated with verbs like "spark", "arouse", or "satisfy" (e.g., to spark curiosity).

Hypothesis
Noun /haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: A proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation.

Vietnamese: Giả thuyết.

Usage: Academic term; hypotheses are typically "proposed", "tested", and either "proven" or "disproven".

Example"The scientists set up an experiment to test their new hypothesis about climate change."
Phenomenon
Noun /fɪˈnɒmɪnən/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: A fact or situation that is observed to exist or happen, especially one whose cause is in question.

Vietnamese: Hiện tượng.

Usage: The plural form is "phenomena". It is widely used to describe observable events in nature or physics.

Example"Gravity is a fundamental physical phenomenon that dictates how planets orbit."
Delve into
Phrasal Verb /dɛlv ˈɪntuː/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: To examine something carefully in order to discover more information about it.

Vietnamese: Đi sâu vào, nghiên cứu kỹ.

Usage: A formal phrasal verb used when actively researching or exploring a complex topic deeply.

Example"If I had the chance, I would love to delve deeply into the subject of quantum mechanics."

Adjective /ˈɪntrɪkət/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: Very complicated or detailed.

Vietnamese: Tinh xảo, phức tạp (nhiều chi tiết).

Usage: Similar to "complex", but emphasizes the presence of many small, detailed, and interconnected parts.

Example"The human immune system is an intricate network of cells and proteins."

Adjective /ˈɡraʊndˌbreɪkɪŋ/
Topic: A Science Subject

Definition: Innovative; pioneering.

Vietnamese: Đột phá, tiên phong.

Usage: Used as an adjective to describe research, studies, or discoveries that fundamentally change how we understand a field.
