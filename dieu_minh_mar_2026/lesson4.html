1. Strategy Explanation (10 mins)

Matching Headings (CD-IELTS UI Focus)

On the computer, students must click and drag headings into the correct slots. It can be visually confusing. Strategy: Always read the Headings list first to prime the brain, then read Paragraph A, find its heading, then Paragraph B, etc.

Topic Sentences: Teach them that the heading is usually found in the first or second sentence of the paragraph (the topic sentence) or occasionally the very last sentence (the conclusion).

Matching Information to Paragraphs

Unlike Headings (which summarize the main idea), Matching Information asks you to find a specific detail (e.g., "a description of...", "a reason why..."). The answer might be hidden in the middle of a paragraph. Don't rely solely on topic sentences for these.

1. Strategy: The Pre-Reading Phase (10 mins)

Part 3 is usually an academic discussion between 2-3 people. The biggest challenge isn't the audio; it's the amount of text you have to read for Long Multiple-Choice questions.

The "Read Before" Rule

  • You get roughly 30-40 seconds before the audio starts. Use every second of it.
  • Do not read the A, B, C options deeply yet. Focus on reading and highlighting the keywords in the questions.
  • You need to know exactly what information you are listening for. The audio will paraphrase the A, B, C options anyway.

Tracking Speakers (Agreement/Disagreement)

Because it's a discussion, Speaker A might suggest an answer, but Speaker B might disagree. Wait for agreement! The correct answer is usually the point they finally agree upon.

2. Live Practice Drill (20 mins)

STEP 1

Give the student the allotted pre-reading time. Ensure they are scanning the questions.

STEP 2

Play the audio. Watch them type/click.

STEP 3

Stop and Review. Discuss the distractors. Did they hear Speaker A suggest an incorrect option before Speaker B corrected them?
