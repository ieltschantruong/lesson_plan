Speaking Part 1

Sports & Physical Exercise

Exercise 1: Vocabulary

I used to hit the gym very often but I came to a realization that strenuous exercise is not something for me. So I've taken up doing yoga instead. This moderate sport can enhance both physical and mental well-being because it helps boost immune system, reduce the risk of some diseases and at same time enhance relaxation. Of course if you do it on a frequent basis, you can get into shape, too. Doing a 20-minute workout everyday can do wonders for your health.
  • 1 hit the gym
    go to the gym to exercise
  • 2 strenuous /ˈstrenjuəs/
    needing great effort/energy
  • 3 take up something
    start a new hobby or habit
  • 4 moderate
    average in amount or intensity
  • 5 well-being
    general health and happiness
  • 6 immune system /ɪˈmjuːn/
    body's defense against disease
  • 7 enhance
    improve or increase
  • 8 get into shape
    become physically fit
  • 9 do a workout
    do a session of physical exercise
  • 10 do wonders
    have a very beneficial effect

Speaking Questions

  1. Do you often do physical exercise?
  2. How do you do exercise?
  3. Do you have any plans for exercise in the future?
  4. Do young people in your country like to do exercise?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. Do you often do physical exercise?

Yes, I try to hit the gym or do a quick 20-minute workout almost every day. It really does wonders for both my physical and mental well-being.

2. How do you do exercise?

I usually prefer moderate activities rather than highly strenuous routines. Because of this, I've recently taken up yoga to enhance my flexibility and relaxation.

3. Do you have any plans for exercise in the future?

I hope to get into shape more effectively, so I might hire a personal trainer. I believe structured training will further boost my immune system.

4. Do young people in your country like to do exercise?

Yes, the younger generation is very health-conscious nowadays. You'll see many of them hitting the gym or joining running clubs to enhance their lifestyles.

Speaking Questions

  1. What is the busiest part of the day for you?
  2. What part of your day do you like best?
  3. Do you usually have the same routine every day?
  4. Do you think it is important to have a daily routine?
  5. What would you like to change in your daily routine?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. What is the busiest part of the day for you?

My mornings are definitely the busiest. I currently have a tight schedule with classes and assignments, making the morning my most productive time.

2. What part of your day do you like best?

I enjoy the late evenings because I finally have some time on my hands to relax. I am a bit of a night owl, so I feel very peaceful at night.

3. Do you usually have the same routine every day?

Yes, I really try to stick to a routine. A typical day of mine starts as an early riser at 6 a.m., followed by breakfast and diving into work.

4. Do you think it is important to have a daily routine?

Absolutely. Making study your main priority and being an organized person helps you arrange your time wisely and avoid feeling overwhelmed.

5. What would you like to change in your daily routine?

I would love to wake up earlier to be a true early riser. This would allow me to be more productive rather than rushing through a tight schedule.

Speaking Part 1

Sleep

Exercise 3: True or False

Are the following statements true or false to you?

STATEMENTS TRUE FALSE
1. I often take a siesta. /siˈestə/
2. I often have a sound sleep at night.
3. Before crucial exams, I toss and turn in bed all night.
4. I'm never sleep-deprived. /dɪˈpraɪvd/
5. I never eat calorific /ˌkæləˈrɪfɪk/ foods before sleep.
6. I often sleep in pitch-black darkness.
7. I'm always awake and alert.
8. I believe that sleeping is beneficial to health.
9. Sleeping is a good way to recharge my batteries.
10. I have the same sleeping pattern every day.
11. Sleeping helps my body function well.
12. I'm a sleep-conscious /ˈkɑːnʃəs/ person.

Speaking Questions

  1. How long should people sleep for the benefit of their health? Why?
  2. Do you think that the elderly should sleep more than young people? Why?
  3. What do you do to improve sleeping quality?
  4. Do you think it is important to have a sleeping routine?
  5. Do you ever take a nap during the day?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. How long should people sleep for the benefit of their health? Why?

Generally, 7-8 hours is beneficial to our health because it gives the body enough time to fully recharge its batteries and ensures our organs function well.

2. Do you think that the elderly should sleep more than young people? Why?

Actually, older people often have a lighter sleeping pattern. However, they still need a sound sleep to remain awake and alert during the day.

3. What do you do to improve sleeping quality?

I try to sleep in pitch-black darkness and avoid calorific foods right before bed. This prevents me from tossing and turning all night.

4. Do you think it is important to have a sleeping routine?

Yes, being sleep-conscious and avoiding becoming sleep-deprived is crucial for maintaining long-term physical health.

5. Do you ever take a nap during the day?

Yes, I often take a short siesta in the afternoon to refresh my mind and maintain my energy levels.
