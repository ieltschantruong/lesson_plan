Speaking Part 1

Topic 1: Computers

Exercise 1: Vocabulary

"I've just bought a brand new laptop from Apple, an internationally renowned tech giant. I find it high-speed, lightweight and powerful, so it greatly facilitates my studies, work and recreation. I often use it for word processing, web browsing, information searching and photo design. Well, I would consider myself relatively computer-literate."
  • 1 brand new
    completely new (hoàn toàn mới)
  • 2 renowned
    famous, well-known (nổi tiếng)
  • 3 tech giant
    large tech company (gã khổng lồ CN)
  • 4 high-speed
    operating quickly (tốc độ cao)
  • 5 lightweight
    weighing less than average
  • 6 facilitate
    make an action easier (tạo đk)
  • 7 browse
    look through information on internet
  • 8 computer-literate
    having skills to use computers

Speaking Questions

  1. Do you use computers?
  2. How often do you use your computer/laptop?
  3. What kinds of activities involve using computers?
  4. How important is your computer to you?
  5. What kinds of computer skills do you have?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. Do you use computers?

Yes, absolutely. I use my laptop every single day. Since I'm fairly computer-literate, it has become an indispensable tool for both my studies and entertainment.

2. How often do you use your computer/laptop?

I would say I'm on my laptop for at least six to eight hours a day. I recently bought a brand new, lightweight model from a renowned tech giant, which makes it very easy to carry around and use wherever I go.

3. What kinds of activities involve using computers?

I mostly use it for word processing and browsing the internet to search for information. When I have free time, I also play some high-speed games to unwind.

4. How important is your computer to you?

It's extremely crucial. Honestly, it greatly facilitates my daily routine, and I would feel completely disconnected and unable to fulfill my work responsibilities without it.

5. What kinds of computer skills do you have?

Well, as I mentioned, I consider myself quite computer-literate. I am proficient in basic office software, and I can also do some basic photo design and troubleshooting.

Exercise 3: True or False

  • 1 Emails can substitute letters.

  • 2 Many people are fed up with letters.

  • 3 Many people are bombarded with spam emails.

  • 4 It is common to jot down ideas that pop into your head.

  • 5 Your personality can manifest itself in handwriting.

Speaking Questions

  1. Do you think emails are useful?
  2. Do you think it is a good thing that companies send out spam emails to customers?
  3. Do you think typing can replace handwriting?
  4. Do you often write something by hand?
  5. What are the differences between letters and emails?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. Do you think emails are useful?

Yes, very useful. They are much faster and more efficient than traditional mail, and it's so easy to attach files. In many professional contexts, they perfectly substitute physical letters.

2. Is it good that companies send spam emails?

Not at all. I am really fed up with being bombarded with unwanted spam messages every time I check my inbox. It's incredibly distracting and feels like a waste of time.

3. Do you think typing can replace handwriting?

In many practical scenarios, yes, because typing is faster. However, handwriting still has a more personal touch that allows your personality to manifest itself, which typing lacks.

4. Do you often write something by hand?

Not as often as I used to. Nowadays, I mostly just use a pen to jot down quick ideas that pop into my head during meetings, or perhaps to write greeting cards to express affection.

5. Differences between letters and emails?

Emails are instant, free, and great for quick updates. Letters, on the other hand, take longer to deliver and can be lost, but they are much more meaningful for personal relationships.

Exercise 4: Matching

Speaking Questions

SOCIAL NETWORKING SITES

  1. What social networking websites do you like to use?
  2. What kinds of people do you like to be friends with on those websites?
  3. Is it easy to find real friends on a social networking site?
  4. What kinds of chatting applications or websites do you like to use?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. What social networking websites do you like to use?

I mostly use Facebook and Instagram. I find that they have a very user-friendly interface, which makes them an excellent platform for staying updated with trends.

2. What kinds of people do you like to be friends with on those websites?

I prefer connecting with my close friends and family members. It helps us stay in touch daily, regardless of the geographic distance between us.

3. Is it easy to find real friends on a social networking site?

No, I think it's quite difficult. The virtual world can be full of misleading information, and there's always a risk of being deceived by cyber friends who hide their true identities.

4. What kinds of chatting applications or websites do you like to use?

I rely heavily on WhatsApp and Zalo for messaging. They are fast, secure, and help me manage my communications effectively without the grave problem of constantly being distracted by endless feeds.

Speaking Questions

TEXTING

  1. Do you like texting someone?
  2. Do you think texting is better than making a phone call?

APPLICATIONS

  1. What applications do you often use?
  2. Do you want to create an application of your own in the future?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

TEXTING 1. Do you like texting someone?

Yes, I prefer texting. Since text-based communication tools have proliferated, it's incredibly convenient to send a quick message rather than interrupting someone's day.

TEXTING 2. Do you think texting is better than making a phone call?

It depends on the context. While phone calls have lost their primacy in daily chats, a spoken conversation accomplishes much more when discussing complex issues or in an emergency situation.

APPLICATIONS 1. What applications do you often use?

Besides social media, I heavily rely on digital banking and navigation apps. They have greatly proliferated in recent years and help me accomplish my daily tasks effortlessly.

APPLICATIONS 2. Do you want to create an application of your own in the future?

I would love to. I think creating an app that accomplishes specific educational goals for children could regain primacy over mindless entertainment apps.
