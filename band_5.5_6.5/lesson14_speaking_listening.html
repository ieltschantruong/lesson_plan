Exercise 1: Vocabulary

👨‍🏫 Teacher Note:

Display the text below. Elicit guesses for the bolded words from the whole class before revealing the meanings.

Read the box below and try to guess the meanings of the bolded words/phrases.

"I've just bought a brand new laptop from Apple, an internationally renowned tech giant. I find it high-speed, lightweight and powerful, so it greatly facilitates my studies, work and recreation. I often use it for word processing, web browsing, information searching and photo design. Well, I would consider myself relatively computer-literate."
WORDS/ PHRASES
MEANINGS
  • 1 brand new (a)
    completely new (hoàn toàn mới)
  • 2 renowned (a) /rɪˈnaʊnd/
    famous, well-known (nổi tiếng)
  • 3 tech giant (n)
    a very large technology company (gã khổng lồ công nghệ)
  • 4 high-speed (a)
    operating very quickly (tốc độ cao)
  • 5 lightweight (a)
    weighing less than average (trọng lượng nhẹ)
  • 6 facilitate (v) /fəˈsɪlɪteɪt/
    make an action or process easy or easier (tạo điều kiện thuận lợi)
  • 7 browse (v)
    look through or read information on the internet (lướt web)
  • 8 computer-literate (a) /ˈlɪtərət/
    having sufficient knowledge and skill to use computers (thành thạo máy tính)
Part 1

COMPUTER

  1. Do you use computers?
  2. How often do you use your computer/laptop?
  3. What kinds of activities involve using computers?
  4. How important is your computer to you?
  5. What kinds of computer skills do you have?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. Do you use computers?
Yes, absolutely. I use my laptop every single day for both work and entertainment.

2. How often do you use your computer/laptop?
I would say I'm on my laptop for at least 6 to 8 hours a day. It's an indispensable tool for my daily routine.

3. What kinds of activities involve using computers?
I mainly use it for word processing, browsing the internet to search for information, and occasionally watching movies or playing lightweight games to unwind.

4. How important is your computer to you?
It's crucial. Honestly, I would feel completely disconnected and unable to fulfill my work responsibilities without it.

5. What kinds of computer skills do you have?
I consider myself reasonably computer-literate. I am proficient in basic office software like Word and Excel, and I can troubleshoot minor software issues.

Part 1

EMAIL

  1. Do you think emails are useful?
  2. Do you think it is a good thing that companies send out spam emails to customers?
  3. Do you think typing can replace handwriting?
  4. Do you often write something by hand?
  5. What are the differences between letters and emails?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. Do you think emails are useful?
Yes, very useful. They facilitate quick and professional communication, especially in academic or corporate environments.

2. Do you think it is a good thing that companies send out spam emails to customers?
Not at all. It's quite frustrating to have your inbox bombarded with unwanted promotional messages. It feels like an invasion of privacy.

3. Do you think typing can replace handwriting?
In many practical scenarios, yes, because it's faster and more efficient. However, handwriting still holds a special personal touch that typing lacks.

4. Do you often write something by hand?
Not as often as I used to. Nowadays, I only jot down quick notes that pop into my head or write greeting cards for special occasions.

5. What are the differences between letters and emails?
Emails are instant, free, and allow you to attach files easily. Letters, on the other hand, take time to deliver but are much more personal and meaningful.

Part 1

SOCIAL NETWORKING SITES

  1. What social networking websites do you like to use?
  2. What kinds of people do you like to be friends with on those websites?
  3. Is it easy to find real friends on a social networking site?
  4. What kinds of chatting applications or websites do you like to use?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

1. What social networking websites do you like to use?
I'm quite active on Facebook and Instagram. They are user-friendly platforms for staying in touch with friends.

2. What kinds of people do you like to be friends with on those websites?
I prefer connecting with people I know in real life, like classmates, colleagues, and family members, to keep up with their lives.

3. Is it easy to find real friends on a social networking site?
I don't think so. The virtual world can be deceptive, and it's hard to build genuine trust with someone you've only met online.

4. What kinds of chatting applications or websites do you like to use?
I mostly use Zalo and Messenger. They are extremely convenient for instant messaging and making free video calls.

Part 1

TEXTING & APPLICATIONS

TEXTING

  1. Do you like texting someone?
  2. Do you think texting is better than making a phone call?

APPLICATIONS

  1. What applications do you often use?
  2. Do you want to create an application of your own in the future?
💡 View Sample Answers ▼

TEXTING 1. Do you like texting someone?
Yes, I prefer texting over calling. It allows me to think before I reply and isn't as intrusive as a phone call.

TEXTING 2. Do you think texting is better than making a phone call?
It depends on the situation. For a quick question, texting is great. But in an emergency, a phone call is definitely more efficient.

APPLICATIONS 1. What applications do you often use?
Besides social media, I heavily rely on banking apps for transactions and Google Maps for navigation.

APPLICATIONS 2. Do you want to create an application of your own in the future?
That sounds fascinating, but I lack the programming skills. If I could, I'd create an app to help people manage their daily expenses more easily.

CUE CARD

👨‍🏫 Teacher Note:

Give students exactly 1 minute to prepare notes, then 2 minutes to speak to their partner or to the class.

Describe a website/ an application you like.

You should say:

  • What it is about "WHAT"
  • When you found it "WHEN"
  • How often you use it "HOW OFTEN"
  • Why you like it "WHY"
💡 View Sample Answer (Band 6.0-6.5) ▼
"I'd like to talk about Duolingo, a language-learning application that I am really fond of. It's basically a platform that helps users learn new languages through interactive mini-games and quizzes.

I discovered it about two years ago when I was looking for a free tool to pick up some basic Spanish before a trip.

I use it almost every day, usually for just 15 to 20 minutes during my commute or right before bed.

I really like it because its interface is extremely user-friendly and colorful. Plus, it breaks down complex grammar into bite-sized lessons, making the learning process feel less like a chore and more like a fun game."
